## Narrator Script

Tasks are where planning meets execution. They're the daily actions — the 7 AM training session, the 9 PM writing block, the Tuesday lunch prep — that accumulate over time into milestones, then goals, then roadmaps.

This lesson covers how to create tasks from the Roadmap page, what each field does, and how to think about building a realistic task schedule.

---

### Adding a Task

Navigate to `/dashboard/roadmap`, expand your roadmap and goal, then expand a milestone. You'll see a **+ Add Task** button under the milestone.

Click it. The task creation form opens.

---

### The Task Form

**Date** — The specific calendar date for this task. Not "sometime this week" — a specific day. The date determines when this task appears in your Daily Planner.

**Time** — What time of day you'll do this. Enter it in 24-hour format (e.g., 07:00 for 7 AM, 19:30 for 7:30 PM). This orders your tasks within a day — earlier tasks appear first.

**Activity** — A short title describing what you'll do. This is what you see in the Daily Planner list. Keep it concise but specific: "Morning track session: 6×400m intervals" is better than "Track workout" because it eliminates decision-making when you open the planner.

**Description** (optional) — Detailed notes. Use this for:
- Exercise protocols (sets, reps, rest periods, weights)
- Creative session prompts or goals
- Reference materials, links, or context
- Progress targets to hit during the session

**Tag** — Which category does this task belong to? This should match the goal's category in most cases. The seven options are the same as goals: FITNESS, CREATIVE, SKILL, OUTREACH, LIFESTYLE, MINDSET, FUEL.

**Priority** — High (P1), Medium (P2), or Low (P3). Priorities show in the Daily Planner and help you triage on busy days. If everything is P1, nothing is P1. Use P1 for tasks that absolutely must happen today, P2 for important but flexible, P3 for "nice to have."

---

### Building a Task Schedule

The most effective task planning starts from the milestone's target date and works backward.

For example: if your milestone "Run a sub-4:30 mile" is due June 15, and today is February 1, you have about 4.5 months. A four-day-per-week training schedule would give you roughly 70 training sessions before the target date.

Work backward:
- Week 1–4: Base fitness and form (4 sessions/week × 4 weeks = 16 tasks)
- Week 5–8: Speed introduction (4 × 4 = 16 tasks)
- Week 9–12: Threshold intervals (4 × 4 = 16 tasks)
- Week 13–16: Race-pace preparation (4 × 4 = 16 tasks)
- Week 17–18: Taper and race (6 tasks)

That's roughly 70 tasks. You wouldn't create all 70 at once — you'd create the next 2–4 weeks at a time, then replenish as you go. The Daily Planner shows you what's coming up, and the Roadmap shows you whether the task volume is appropriate for the milestone.

---

### Creating Tasks in Bulk

If you need many similar tasks (like a training schedule), the most efficient approach is to:
1. Create the first task with a complete description
2. Note the milestone it's under
3. Add tasks for the next 2–3 weeks, adjusting the date and any session-specific details

For recurring tasks with the same pattern every week, use the Recurring Tasks feature covered in Lesson 08 — it generates tasks automatically instead of creating them manually.

---

### Editing Tasks

To edit a task, click the **Edit** button on the task row in the Roadmap view, or click the Edit button on the task card in the Daily Planner. The same form opens with current values pre-filled. Update any field and save.

You can change the date (reschedule), time (adjust your schedule), activity name, description, priority, or any other field. All changes take effect immediately.

## Key Takeaways

- Tasks require: Date, Time, Activity, Tag, and Priority — description is optional but highly recommended
- Date and Time determine when the task appears in the Daily Planner
- Activity name should be specific enough to eliminate decision-making at execution time
- Use description for protocols, notes, targets, or anything you'll want at execution time
- Build task schedules by working backward from milestone target dates
- For recurring patterns (daily training, weekly sessions), use Recurring Tasks (Lesson 08)

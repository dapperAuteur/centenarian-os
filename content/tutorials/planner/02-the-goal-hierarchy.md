## Narrator Script

Before you create anything in the Planner, you need to understand the four-level hierarchy that everything is built on. Get this mental model right, and the whole system will feel intuitive. Skip it, and you'll waste time building things in the wrong places.

The four levels are: **Roadmap → Goal → Milestone → Task**. Each one answers a different question, operates at a different timescale, and has a different purpose.

---

### Level 1: The Roadmap

A roadmap is your longest-range container. It represents a chapter of your life — a period during which you're pursuing a coherent set of goals.

**The question it answers:** "What am I trying to accomplish across the next 5, 10, or 25 years?"

A roadmap has a title, an optional description, and a start and end year. For example: "Centenarian Journey 2025–2050" or "Athletic Peak 2025–2035".

Most people only have one or two roadmaps. You might have one for your personal health and longevity journey, and another for a business or creative project with a separate time horizon.

A roadmap is the anchor. Everything else lives inside it.

---

### Level 2: Goals

Goals live inside a roadmap. Each goal is a major life objective — something worth pursuing over months to years.

**The question it answers:** "What specific outcomes do I want to achieve by a certain year?"

A goal has a title, a target year, and a category (FITNESS, CREATIVE, SKILL, OUTREACH, LIFESTYLE, MINDSET, or FUEL). Examples:
- "Run a sub-4:00 mile" — FITNESS goal, target year: 2027
- "Publish a novel" — CREATIVE goal, target year: 2028
- "Learn conversational Japanese" — SKILL goal, target year: 2026

Goals can be set to active, completed, deferred, or archived. When you hit one, mark it completed and move on to the next.

---

### Level 3: Milestones

Milestones live inside a goal. Each milestone is a measurable checkpoint on the way to that goal.

**The question it answers:** "What specific, verifiable things need to happen, and by when, for me to achieve this goal?"

A milestone has a title, a specific target date, and a status: not started, in progress, completed, or blocked.

Good milestones are:
- **Time-bound** — a specific date, not "someday"
- **Binary** — either done or not done (no ambiguity)
- **Sequenced** — earlier milestones enable later ones

For the "Run a sub-4:00 mile" goal, milestones might be:
- Q2 2025: Run a sub-5:00 mile
- Q4 2025: Run a sub-4:30 mile
- Q2 2026: Run a sub-4:10 mile
- Q4 2026: Run a sub-4:00 mile

Each milestone is a measurable waypoint. The goal is the destination; milestones are the route markers.

---

### Level 4: Tasks

Tasks live inside a milestone. Each task is a specific action you'll do on a specific date and time.

**The question it answers:** "What do I actually do today, this week, or this month to move toward this milestone?"

A task has:
- **Activity** — what you're doing (e.g., "Morning track session: 6×400m @ 70% effort")
- **Date** — when you'll do it
- **Time** — what time of day
- **Tag** — which category it belongs to
- **Priority** — High (P1), Medium (P2), or Low (P3)
- **Description** (optional) — detailed notes, instructions, sets/reps, focus

Tasks are the daily executable units. They're what you see in the Daily Planner view. They're what you check off. They're what moves the needle.

---

### Why the Hierarchy Matters

The hierarchy creates two things that most planning systems don't: **traceability** and **accountability**.

**Traceability** — Every task you create is connected to a milestone, which is connected to a goal, which is connected to a roadmap. You always know *why* you're doing something. This matters when willpower is low and you need to remember the bigger picture.

**Accountability** — The hierarchy creates natural review points. When you do your weekly review, you can see: are my tasks progressing my milestones? Are my milestones on track to hit my goals? If a milestone is "blocked", what tasks would unblock it?

The system doesn't just track what you're doing — it tracks whether what you're doing is actually moving you forward.

---

### A Quick Design Note

You don't need to have everything figured out before you start. Start with one roadmap. Add one or two goals. Add one milestone per goal. Add a few tasks.

The hierarchy grows as you plan more. Most people find that the act of filling in the hierarchy reveals gaps in their thinking — "wait, I don't actually know how I'm going to get from here to there." That clarity is the point.

## Key Takeaways

- **Roadmap** — your life chapter (5–25 years); answers "what am I pursuing long-term?"
- **Goal** — a major outcome in a specific year; tagged by life area; can be completed, deferred, or archived
- **Milestone** — a binary checkpoint with a specific due date; status: not started / in progress / completed / blocked
- **Task** — a specific daily action; has a date, time, tag, and priority; lives in the Daily Planner
- Every task traces back to a goal — that's what makes daily work feel meaningful
- Start simple: one roadmap, two goals, one milestone each, a few tasks — build from there

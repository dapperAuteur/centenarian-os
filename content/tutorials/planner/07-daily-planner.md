## Narrator Script

The Roadmap page is where you build your plan. The Daily Planner is where you live it. It's your operational view — what needs to happen today, this week, or this month — and it's the interface you'll interact with every single day.

This lesson covers everything about using the Daily Planner effectively: the view modes, task completion, and how to make the planner a genuine daily habit.

---

### Navigating to the Daily Planner

Go to `/dashboard/planner`. You'll see your tasks organized by date, starting from today.

If you've created tasks on the Roadmap page, they appear here automatically — no extra steps needed. The planner is a live view of all your tasks, regardless of which milestone or goal they belong to.

---

### View Modes

At the top of the planner, you'll find a view mode toggle with three options:

**Day** — Shows tasks for a single day. Good for focus: see only what's on today's plate. If you have many tasks and want to stay in a narrow window, Day mode keeps it simple.

**Week** — Shows tasks from Monday through Sunday of the selected week. This is the most popular view for most users — enough context to see the week's shape without being overwhelming. You can see which days are heavy and which are light.

**Month** — Shows tasks for the entire calendar month. Useful for periodic planning sessions: step back, see the full month, and identify gaps, overloads, or upcoming milestone dates.

Toggle between modes using the Day/Week/Month buttons. The date picker adjusts automatically to match the selected period.

---

### Navigating Dates

Use the **date picker** to jump to any specific date. Change the view mode and the date picker together to see, say, "the week of March 10" or "next month."

For the Day view, clicking forward or backward moves by one day. For Week, it moves a full week. For Month, a full month.

---

### Completing Tasks

Each task card has a **checkbox** on the left side. Check it when you've completed the task.

When a task is checked:
- The activity text gets a strikethrough
- The task is marked complete in the database with a `completed_at` timestamp
- The progress bar at the top updates immediately
- The completion count ("X / Y tasks") increments

The progress bar shows your completion percentage for the current view period. For Day mode, it shows today's progress. For Week mode, it shows the week's completion percentage.

This is the daily feedback loop: open the planner in the morning, see what's scheduled, work through the day, and watch the progress bar fill up.

---

### Task Cards in the Planner

Each task card shows:

**Tag badge** — The colored category (FITNESS, CREATIVE, etc.) so you can see at a glance what kind of work this is.

**Priority** — P1, P2, or P3. P1 items are shown with higher visual emphasis — they're what you must do today.

**Financial info** (if set) — If a task has estimated cost or revenue, it shows in green (+$X) or red (-$X) inline. See Lesson 09 for more on financial tracking.

**Activity name** — What you're doing, with strikethrough if completed.

**Time** — When the task is scheduled, with a clock icon.

**Expand toggle** — Click to show the full description. If you wrote a detailed session protocol in the description, it appears here.

**Edit button** — Opens the edit form for this task. Change date, time, activity, description, priority, or any other field.

---

### Financial Summary

At the bottom of the Planner (or in a summary panel), you'll see a summary for the current view period:
- **Estimated Cost** — total estimated cost of all tasks in the period
- **Actual Cost** — total actual cost logged
- **Revenue** — total revenue associated with tasks
- **Net Profit** — revenue minus actual cost (green if positive, red if negative)

This is useful for tracking project economics. If you're building a business and tagging tasks with their costs and revenue, the Planner summary gives you a live financial picture.

---

### Building a Daily Habit with the Planner

The Daily Planner only works if you actually open it. The users who get the most value from it have a morning routine built around it:

1. Open the Daily Planner (Day view)
2. Review what's scheduled for today
3. Note any P1 tasks that must happen — those are non-negotiable
4. Throughout the day, check tasks off as you complete them
5. End of day: see the progress bar and reflect on what got done

Some people also do a brief evening review in Week mode: look at what they completed this week, see what's coming up tomorrow, and adjust any tasks that need to move.

The planner is only as useful as the habits around it. Two minutes in the morning and two minutes in the evening is enough to get significant value.

## Key Takeaways

- Three view modes: Day (today's focus), Week (the week's shape), Month (big picture)
- Check the checkbox to mark a task complete — strikethrough + progress bar update instantly
- Completion percentage at the top shows your progress for the current view period
- P1/P2/P3 priorities help you triage on busy days — always protect your P1 tasks
- Tag badges, financial info, and the expand toggle are all in the task card
- Two-minute morning + two-minute evening check-in is all it takes to use the planner effectively

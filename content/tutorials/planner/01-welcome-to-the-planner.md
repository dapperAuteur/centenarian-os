## Narrator Script

Most planning systems fail for the same reason: they only operate at one timescale. You either have a list of tasks for today, or a vague set of long-term goals — but nothing connecting the two. The Planner in CentenarianOS is built to solve that gap.

It operates across four levels simultaneously, from the scale of decades down to the scale of a single morning. Every task you check off is connected, by design, to something larger than today.

In this lesson, we'll take a quick tour of the module and explain the philosophy before you start building.

---

### What the Planner Is For

The Planner is for people who have meaningful long-term goals — the kind that take years or decades to achieve — and who want daily accountability without losing sight of the big picture.

A roadmap might span 10–25 years. A goal inside that roadmap might target a specific year. A milestone might be due in six months. And a task might be on your calendar for 7 AM tomorrow. All of these are linked. When you complete tomorrow's task, it contributes to the milestone, which contributes to the goal, which moves the roadmap forward.

That's the operating system model: every action is purposeful, traceable, and connected to something that matters.

---

### The Three Pages

The Planner module has three main pages, each with a different scope:

**Roadmap** (`/dashboard/roadmap`) — The big picture. This is where you create and manage your roadmap, add goals, set milestones, and see the entire hierarchy in one expandable tree view. You use this page to structure your long-term plan and periodically review whether everything is set up correctly.

**Daily Planner** (`/dashboard/planner`) — The everyday view. This shows your tasks for today, this week, or this month. It's your operating dashboard: check tasks off as you complete them, see your completion percentage for the day, and manage the work in front of you. Most days, this is the only page you need to visit.

**Weekly Review** (`/dashboard/weekly-review`) — The reflection layer. Once a week, generate an AI-written summary of how your week went: tasks completed, nutrition quality, focus sessions, travel, health metrics, and more. It ends with one actionable focus for the coming week.

---

### Tags: The Common Language

Throughout the Planner, everything is tagged using a shared set of seven categories:

- **FITNESS** — physical training, movement, recovery
- **CREATIVE** — writing, design, music, art
- **SKILL** — learning, professional development
- **OUTREACH** — community, relationships, teaching, networking
- **LIFESTYLE** — habits, environment, daily systems
- **MINDSET** — mental health, journaling, meditation
- **FUEL** — nutrition, meal prep, cooking

These tags connect goals, milestones, and tasks across the hierarchy. A FITNESS goal has FITNESS milestones and FITNESS tasks. The tags make it easy to filter your day to just the work you need to do, and they feed the analytics that show how your time is distributed across life areas.

## Key Takeaways

- The Planner connects decades to days: Roadmap → Goal → Milestone → Task
- Three pages: Roadmap (structure), Daily Planner (execution), Weekly Review (reflection)
- Seven shared tags (FITNESS, CREATIVE, SKILL, etc.) unify the hierarchy and power analytics
- Every daily task is traceable back to a long-term goal — nothing is disconnected from purpose

# Lesson 03: Creating Your Roadmap

**Course:** Mastering the Planner

## Narrator Script

A roadmap is the container for everything else in your plan. Before you can add goals, milestones, or tasks, you need at least one roadmap. This lesson walks through creating one, what the fields mean, and how to think about your time horizon.

---

### Navigating to the Roadmap Page

Go to `/dashboard/roadmap`. If you're new to the Planner, you'll see an empty state with a prompt to create your first roadmap. If you've already set up a roadmap, you'll see it displayed as a sky-blue card.

Click **+ New Roadmap** to open the creation form.

---

### The Roadmap Form

**Title** — The name of this roadmap. Think of it as the name of a life chapter. Examples:
- "My Centenarian Journey"
- "Athletic Peak Project"
- "Building the Business 2025–2030"

Keep it meaningful to you — you'll see this name every time you open the Planner.

**Description** (optional) — A few sentences describing what this roadmap is about. Useful if you have multiple roadmaps and want to remember the focus of each.

**Start date** — When does this chapter begin? For most users, this is today or the beginning of the current year.

**End date** — When do you envision this chapter completing? For a longevity-focused roadmap, you might put 2050 or 2075. For a business sprint, it might be 3–5 years from now. Don't overthink this — you can always update the end date later.

---

### How to Think About Your Time Horizon

Most people underestimate how much they can accomplish over 10–25 years and overestimate what they can do in a single year. A multi-decade roadmap forces you to zoom out.

A useful framing: what would your life look like if you made consistent, deliberate progress for the next 15 years? What would your health be like? What skills would you have? What would you have built or created? The roadmap is where you write that answer down.

You don't need to have every goal figured out when you create the roadmap. The title, description, and time horizon are enough to start. Goals come next.

---

### Saving the Roadmap

Click **Save**. The roadmap card appears on your page with the sky-blue gradient header. It will be empty — no goals yet — but it's the foundation your whole plan sits on.

---

### Multiple Roadmaps

You can have more than one roadmap. Some users have:
- A personal health roadmap (long horizon)
- A business or career roadmap (shorter horizon)
- A creative project roadmap (could be 2–5 years)

Each roadmap is completely separate — its own goals, milestones, and tasks. If you have genuinely separate life projects that don't share goals, separate roadmaps make sense. If everything is connected (you want to be healthy so you can build the business, which funds the lifestyle that supports longevity), one roadmap might be better.

---

### Editing a Roadmap

To change a roadmap's title, description, or dates, click the **Edit** button on the roadmap card. The same form opens with your current values. Update and save.

## Key Takeaways

- A roadmap is the top-level container; everything else (goals, milestones, tasks) nests inside it
- Required: Title, Start date, End date — description is optional
- Multi-decade time horizons are the point — don't constrain your vision to a year or two
- You can have multiple roadmaps for genuinely separate life chapters
- Edit at any time — time horizons change, and that's expected

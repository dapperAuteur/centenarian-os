## Narrator Script

A goal without milestones is just a wish. Milestones are the bridge between the aspirational and the executable — they're the specific, time-bound checkpoints that tell you whether you're on track.

This lesson covers how to create milestones, what makes a good one, and how to use milestone status to actively manage your progress.

---

### Adding a Milestone

Navigate to `/dashboard/roadmap` and expand your roadmap, then expand a goal. You'll see a **+ Add Milestone** button under the goal.

Click it. The milestone form opens.

---

### The Milestone Form

**Title** — A specific, verifiable checkpoint. Not "improve running" but "Run a sub-4:30 mile." Not "make progress on the novel" but "Complete a full first draft (80,000 words)."

Good milestone titles describe the outcome, not the activity. The activity is what you'll put in tasks. The milestone is what you'll have achieved.

**Description** (optional) — More context: why this milestone, what it unlocks, what it means for the goal.

**Target date** — A specific calendar date by which this milestone will be reached. This is more precise than a goal's target year — milestones should have quarter, month, or week-level precision. Examples:
- March 31, 2026
- June 15, 2025
- December 1, 2027

If you can't assign a target date, the milestone isn't ready. Break it down further or gather more information first.

**Status** — Start with "Not Started." The full progression is:
- **Not Started** — hasn't begun
- **In Progress** — actively working toward it
- **Completed** — done
- **Blocked** — you can't move forward until something else happens (waiting on a resource, an external dependency, recovery from injury, etc.)

---

### What Makes a Good Milestone

The best milestones are:

**Binary** — Either done or not done. "Complete a half marathon" is binary. "Improve my running" is not. You can't be "mostly done" with a binary milestone — clarity is the point.

**Achievable in 1–6 months** — Milestones that stretch longer than 6 months are often goals in disguise. If a milestone would take 2 years, it's probably a goal itself. Break it into 3–4 quarterly milestones instead.

**Sequenced logically** — Earlier milestones should enable later ones. For the sub-4:00 mile goal, the milestone sequence would be: sub-5:00, then sub-4:30, then sub-4:10, then sub-4:00. Each one proves capacity for the next.

**Preceded by a sprint of tasks** — Every milestone should have tasks that specifically prepare for it. If you can't describe what you'd do in the next two weeks to move a milestone toward completion, something is wrong with either the milestone or your planning.

---

### Using Status Actively

The status field isn't just a label — it's a management tool.

Update milestones to **In Progress** when you've started generating tasks for them. This signals that this milestone is live, not hypothetical.

Mark milestones as **Completed** when you've hit the target. The completion timestamp is recorded — over time, you can look back and see exactly when you achieved each checkpoint on the way to a goal.

Use **Blocked** when you genuinely can't move forward. Name the blocker in the description so you can address it. A blocked milestone might need a new prerequisite milestone ("Get clearance from physical therapist") before it can resume.

---

### How Many Milestones Per Goal?

For a 2–3 year goal, aim for 4–8 milestones total. That's roughly one milestone every 3–6 months.

Too few milestones and you lose the feedback loop — you won't know if you're off track until it's too late. Too many and the milestone layer becomes noisy, with milestones indistinguishable from tasks.

A useful rule: if you can do a milestone in a single week, it's probably a task. If it would take more than a year, it's probably a goal.

## Key Takeaways

- Milestones are binary, time-bound checkpoints between goals and tasks
- Title should describe the outcome, not the activity
- Target date must be a specific calendar date — no "someday"
- Status: Not Started → In Progress → Completed (or Blocked when stuck)
- Best milestones: binary, 1–6 months in scope, sequenced so each one enables the next
- Aim for 4–8 milestones per 2–3 year goal — enough for a feedback loop, not so many they become noise

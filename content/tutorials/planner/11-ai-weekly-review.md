## Narrator Script

Execution without reflection is just busyness. The weekly review is where you step back, see the full picture of how your week went, and get one clear focus for the week ahead. In CentenarianOS, this review is generated by Gemini — an AI that reads your actual data across every module and writes a personalized coaching summary.

---

### What the Weekly Review Is

Once a week (typically Sunday evening or Monday morning), you generate a review. The system pulls data from every module you've been using — tasks, nutrition, focus sessions, health metrics, travel, courses — and synthesizes it into a 300–450 word plain-text summary.

It covers:
- **Energy & Recovery** — sleep, resting heart rate, HRV, pain levels, energy ratings
- **Focus & Productivity** — focus sessions logged, minutes of deep work, quality ratings, task revenue
- **Nutrition** — meals logged, Green/Yellow/Red NCV distribution, restaurant vs. home-cooked ratio
- **Tasks** — total scheduled, total completed, completion percentage
- **Learning** — lessons completed in courses, watch time, what you studied
- **Travel** — bike miles logged, commute days, CO2 saved, car miles, fuel cost

The review ends with **one actionable focus for the coming week** — a specific, grounded recommendation based on what the data shows.

---

### Navigating to Weekly Review

Go to `/dashboard/weekly-review`. You'll see:
- A week selector with navigation arrows (< >)
- A **Generate Review** button
- The current week's review (if you've generated one)
- An archive of past reviews on the side

---

### Generating Your First Review

Make sure you're on the week you want to review. The default is the current week, but you can navigate backward to review past weeks too.

Click **Generate Review**.

The system makes 8 parallel data queries across all your modules, then sends the aggregated data to Gemini with a coaching prompt. This typically takes 30–60 seconds.

When the review appears, read it all the way through. It will reference specific numbers from your week: "You completed 14 of 21 scheduled tasks (67%)" or "Your nutrition averaged Yellow NCV for the week with 3 restaurant meals." These aren't generic motivational statements — they're grounded in your actual data.

---

### The Review Structure

A typical weekly review covers six sections in sequence:

1. **Energy & Recovery** — how your body held up (sleep quality, pain levels, HRV trends)
2. **Focus & Productivity** — time in deep work and task completion rate
3. **Nutrition** — meal quality and eating patterns
4. **Tasks & Milestones** — progress toward goals
5. **Learning** — course and skill progress
6. **Travel & Activity** — movement, commutes, bike savings

If you didn't log any data in a module that week (e.g., no health metrics logged), the review will note the gap and suggest that tracking it would improve future reviews. It flags zero or null data rather than ignoring it.

---

### The Actionable Focus

The final paragraph of every review gives you one concrete focus for the coming week. This might be:
- "Your task completion rate dropped to 52% — focus on protecting your P1 tasks and deferring P3s when time is tight"
- "Nutrition was mostly Yellow with significant restaurant meals — consider logging one more home-cooked protocol meal per day"
- "You had no focus sessions this week — schedule at least two 45-minute deep work blocks as P1 tasks for Monday and Wednesday"

The focus is based on what your data actually shows, not generic advice. This is the most actionable part of the review.

---

### Past Reviews

Your generated reviews are stored. The archive sidebar on the review page lists every week you've generated a review for — click any date to load that week's review.

Over time, the archive becomes a journal of your progress. Looking back at reviews from three months ago and comparing them to now is one of the most motivating experiences the system offers. The numbers change. The patterns become visible.

---

### When to Do It

Most users generate their review on Sunday evening or first thing Monday morning. The best time is when you have 5–10 minutes to actually read it, not just generate it and close the tab.

The review is most valuable when you take the actionable focus seriously and act on it during the coming week. If you consistently generate reviews but ignore the focus, the pattern will eventually show up in the data itself.

## Key Takeaways

- The weekly review is an AI-generated (Gemini) coaching summary based on your actual data from all modules
- Covers: energy/recovery, focus/productivity, nutrition, tasks, learning, and travel
- Generation takes 30–60 seconds; pulls from 8 data sources in parallel
- The final paragraph gives one concrete actionable focus for the coming week — based on real data, not generic advice
- Past reviews are archived — reading them over time shows your long-term progress
- Best done Sunday evening or Monday morning — read it fully and act on the focus this week

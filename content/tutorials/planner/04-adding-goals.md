## Narrator Script

Goals are the outcomes that justify the roadmap. They're specific, meaningful achievements you're working toward — not vague intentions, but concrete targets with a category and a target year.

This lesson covers how to add goals to your roadmap, what the fields mean, and how to think about designing good goals.

---

### Adding a Goal

Navigate to `/dashboard/roadmap` and expand your roadmap by clicking on it (or the chevron). Inside the roadmap, you'll see a **+ Add Goal** button.

Click it. The goal creation form opens.

---

### The Goal Form

**Title** — What specifically do you want to achieve? Be concrete. "Get in shape" is not a goal — it's a wish. "Achieve a sub-4:00 mile run" is a goal. "Publish a novel by December 2027" is a goal.

The more specific the title, the easier it is to know when you've achieved it. Vague titles lead to milestone and task ambiguity down the line.

**Description** (optional) — More context on this goal. Why does it matter? What does achieving it look like? How does it connect to the roadmap?

**Category** — Choose from the seven tags:
- FITNESS — physical performance, body composition, endurance
- CREATIVE — writing, music, design, art, building
- SKILL — languages, technical skills, professional capabilities
- OUTREACH — community involvement, teaching, mentoring, relationships
- LIFESTYLE — environment, daily systems, housing, travel
- MINDSET — meditation, therapy, journaling, emotional regulation
- FUEL — nutrition mastery, cooking skills, food quality

The category affects how the goal is displayed and how it's aggregated in analytics. A FITNESS goal will generate FITNESS milestones and tasks.

**Target year** — The year by which you expect to achieve this goal. This is a commitment, not a wish. If you're not willing to say a year, the goal isn't real yet — it's still an idea.

---

### Designing Goals That Work

The most common mistake in goal-setting: goals that are too vague to generate specific milestones.

A good test: can you name three milestones right now that would clearly indicate you're on track for this goal? If yes, the goal is specific enough. If no, narrow it down.

Another test: could someone other than you verify that you've achieved this goal? "Be healthier" can't be verified. "Run a 5K in under 25 minutes" can. Verifiability keeps you honest.

**Time horizon alignment:** goals should be achievable within the roadmap's time frame. If your roadmap ends in 2040 and your goal targets 2035, that's well-aligned. If your goal targets 2060 in a roadmap that ends in 2050, consider either extending the roadmap or adjusting the target year.

---

### Goal Status

Goals start as **active**. Over time, you can update the status:

- **Active** — you're working on it
- **Completed** — you achieved it (congratulations!)
- **Deferred** — you've paused it; the goal still matters but it's on hold
- **Archived** — you've decided not to pursue it anymore; it will be removed from the active view

Use Deferred for temporary pauses (injured and can't train, project pivoted, life circumstances changed). Use Completed when you've actually hit the target. Archive goals you've decided to abandon entirely.

---

### Multiple Goals Per Roadmap

A roadmap can have many goals. Across different categories, this is expected — you're not one-dimensional. A healthy roadmap might have:
- 2–3 FITNESS goals
- 1–2 SKILL goals
- 1 CREATIVE goal
- 1–2 LIFESTYLE goals

Don't add more goals than you can actively work toward simultaneously. Each active goal needs milestones and tasks. If you have 20 active goals but can only realistically work on 4–6 at once, you'll create a cluttered plan with no focus. Add goals when you're ready to generate milestones for them.

## Key Takeaways

- Goals answer: "What specific outcomes do I want to achieve by a certain year?"
- Title should be concrete and verifiable — you must be able to know when you've hit it
- Seven categories: FITNESS, CREATIVE, SKILL, OUTREACH, LIFESTYLE, MINDSET, FUEL
- Target year is a commitment — if you can't name a year, the goal isn't ready
- Status: active → completed when done; deferred for pauses; archived to remove from view
- Don't create more goals than you can generate milestones for — focus wins over breadth

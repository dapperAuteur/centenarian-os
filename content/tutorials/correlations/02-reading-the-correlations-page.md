## Narrator Script

The Correlations page is where your data becomes visual. This lesson walks through every element of the page and explains what you're looking at.

---

### Time Range Filter

At the top right of the Correlations page, three buttons let you set the analysis window: **7d**, **30d**, **90d**.

- **7 days** — the most recent week. Very small sample (7 data points max per metric pair). Coefficients will be unreliable but useful for spotting very recent pattern shifts.
- **30 days** — one month. The most useful default for most users. Enough data to produce meaningful coefficients while remaining recent enough to be actionable.
- **90 days** — three months. The strongest statistical reliability. Best for understanding your longer-term patterns.

When you change the range, the page re-fetches data and all scatter plots update. The AI insights also regenerate based on the new range.

---

### AI Insights Section

At the top of the page, before the scatter plots, up to three AI Insight cards are shown — but only if the analysis found correlations worth reporting. Each card:

- Has a teal left border accent
- Contains a single plain-language insight generated by Gemini from the strongest correlations found in your data
- Includes a specific actionable suggestion

Example: *"On days when you slept 7+ hours, your energy was 23% higher. Consider setting a consistent bedtime to maximize your alertness."*

These insights are generated fresh each time you load the page or change the time range. They describe your data specifically — not generic health advice. If you see advice that matches a pattern you've already noticed, that's validation. If it surprises you, that's a discovery worth acting on.

If the analysis finds fewer than 3 noteworthy correlations, fewer than 3 insight cards will appear. If there's not enough data, the section is hidden entirely.

---

### Metric Correlations — The Scatter Plot Grid

Below the insights, a grid of scatter plot cards shows every metric pair that had enough data to analyze. Each card:

**Header:**
- Left: "{Metric A} vs {Metric B}" — the two metrics being compared
- Right: The correlation coefficient in large bold text (e.g., **+0.65**), color-coded by strength:
  - **Green** — Strong (r ≥ 0.70)
  - **Amber** — Moderate (r ≥ 0.40)
  - **Gray** — Weak (r < 0.40)
- Below the coefficient: the strength label ("Strong", "Moderate", "Weak") and the sample size ("14 days")

**The Chart:**
- Each dot represents one day where both metrics were logged
- **X-axis** — Metric A (labeled at the bottom)
- **Y-axis** — Metric B (labeled on the left, rotated)
- **Hover over any dot** — a tooltip shows the exact values for that day

**Reading the pattern:**
- Dots trending up-right → positive correlation (more A = more B)
- Dots trending down-right → negative correlation (more A = less B)
- Dots scattered randomly → weak or no correlation

---

### The Six Metric Pairs

The system tests these six specific combinations:

| Metric A | Metric B | What a strong positive r means |
|----------|----------|-------------------------------|
| Sleep Hours | Energy Rating | More sleep → higher daily energy |
| Daily Steps | Energy Rating | More movement → higher energy |
| Pain Intensity | Focus Minutes | Higher pain → less focused work (negative r expected) |
| Green Meal % | Energy Rating | Better nutrition → higher energy |
| Sleep Hours | Focus Minutes | More sleep → more focused work |
| Resting HR | Energy Rating | Lower RHR → higher energy (negative r expected) |

Not all six will appear every time — a pair is only shown if there are at least 5 days where both metrics were logged on the same day. If you've been logging health metrics but not the Engine daily debrief, Energy Rating pairs won't have data.

---

### Empty State

If you have fewer than 5 days of overlapping logged data across any metric pair, you'll see an empty state: "Not enough data yet. Continue logging health metrics, focus sessions, and meals for at least 5 days to detect patterns."

This is the only reason the page shows nothing — it's not a technical error. Keep logging consistently and check back.

## Key Takeaways

- Time range: 7d (recent, small sample), 30d (best default), 90d (most reliable)
- AI Insights: up to 3 Gemini-generated plain-language findings from the strongest correlations
- Each scatter plot card shows: metric pair title, r coefficient (color-coded), strength label, sample size, interactive chart
- Hover any data point for exact values on that day
- Six metric pairs tested: sleep↔energy, steps↔energy, pain↔focus, green meals↔energy, sleep↔focus, RHR↔energy
- A pair only shows if both metrics were logged on the same day — at least 5 overlapping days required

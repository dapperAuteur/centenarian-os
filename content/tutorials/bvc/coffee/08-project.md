# Project — Trace Your Favorite Coffee

*Better Vice Club Episode 1 · Out-of-class project · 2–3 weeks · Pick ONE option*

This project replaces a traditional essay. Pick the option that fits your interests and available time. Each option takes 2–3 weeks of focused work and is graded against a published rubric (see Teacher Resources on Lesson 1 for full rubrics).

**Deliverables submit through the assignment system on this lesson.**

---

## Option A — My Morning Economics Portfolio *(Economics focus)*

Build a multimedia portfolio analyzing the economics of your daily morning routine using coffee as the model.

**Requirements:**

1. Map the supply chain of 3 items from your morning routine (at least one non-coffee item for comparison)
2. Calculate the economic footprint of your morning choices for one month (receipts, photos, estimates — real numbers)
3. Compare costs/impacts of different choices: local vs. chain, premium vs. commodity, home-brew vs. café
4. Design your ideal morning routine based on your economic values and budget — justify with evidence from your data

**Deliverables:** Supply-chain maps (visual — can be hand-drawn, Figma, whatever), cost-benefit analysis spreadsheet, 5-minute presentation recording, 500-word reflection.

**Assessment weighting:** Economic analysis 40% · Research quality 30% · Critical thinking 20% · Presentation 10%.

---

## Option B — Coffee Belt Climate Report *(Geography focus)*

Research and present a climate-vulnerability assessment for one coffee-producing country. Pick any country from the Top 10 in Lesson 2, or go deeper on a smaller producer.

**Requirements:**

1. Document current climate conditions in coffee-growing regions of your chosen country (temperature, rainfall, elevation)
2. Research projected climate changes by 2050 using **at least 3 peer-reviewed sources**
3. Identify adaptation strategies already being implemented there (by farmers, co-ops, or government)
4. Assess the likelihood of continued coffee viability in that country. Defend your assessment.

**Deliverables:** Written report 1,000–1,500 words, with maps, data tables, and APA bibliography.

**Assessment weighting:** Data accuracy 35% · Source quality 25% · Analysis depth 25% · Presentation 15%.

---

## Option C — Community Coffee Stories *(ELA focus)*

Document coffee culture in your community through interviews and observation. Follow the **Interview Ethics Guide** in Lesson 5 carefully.

**Requirements:**

1. Conduct 2–3 interviews with people about their coffee rituals (informed consent, right to review)
2. Write an observation piece based on 30 minutes spent in a local café or coffee-related space — descriptive, not evaluative
3. Analyze one coffee advertisement using the **Media Literacy framework** from Lesson 5 (who's shown, who's absent, what story, what techniques, what gap)
4. Write a reflective essay connecting your findings to themes from the podcast episode

**Deliverables:** Interview transcripts with consent documentation, observation narrative 500 words, ad analysis 300 words, reflective essay 500 words.

**Assessment weighting:** Interview quality 30% · Writing craft 30% · Analysis 25% · Ethics/process 15%.

---

## Option D — Coffeehouse to Congress *(Social Studies focus)*

Research the role of public gathering spaces in democratic movements, using coffee's history as your starting point.

**Requirements:**

1. Document at least **3 historical examples** of coffeehouses/cafés influencing political change (can include examples beyond what Lesson 3 covered — find your own)
2. Identify a modern equivalent (community space, online forum, neighborhood meeting, group chat, etc.) and compare its democratic function
3. Analyze at least **2 primary sources** from different centuries
4. Argue whether modern public spaces serve the same democratic function as historical coffeehouses. Include a **counterargument section** addressing the strongest objection to your thesis.

**Deliverables:** Research paper 1,200–1,500 words, primary source citations, APA bibliography, counterargument section.

**Assessment weighting:** Historical evidence 35% · Primary source analysis 25% · Argument quality 25% · Writing 15%.

## Two essay-style questions for your project reflection

Regardless of which option you pick, address these two questions somewhere in your reflection:

- **Q:** Trace the word "coffee" through at least four languages and explain what the trail reveals.
- **Q:** How does the Ethiopian coffee ceremony mirror three-act narrative structure?

(The master doc's knowledge check has short answers to both, but the goal here is *your* reflection, not recall.)

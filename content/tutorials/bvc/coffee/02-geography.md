# Geography — Where Coffee Grows

Coffee is one of the most geographically precise crops on Earth. The map on this page is your primary tool — zoom, pan, click any marker.

## The Coffee Belt

Coffee grows in a band around the equator stretching from **25°N to 30°S** — the "Coffee Belt." 81 countries across three continents produce coffee commercially (ICO, 2024).

**Regional distribution (2024):**

- South America: 41%
- Southeast Asia: 27%
- Africa: 17%
- Central America: 10%
- Other: 5%

## What coffee needs to grow

**Arabica** (70% of global production) — the species most people drink:

- Temperature: 15–24°C (60–70°F) · damage above 30°C, death below 0°C
- Annual rainfall: 1,500–2,000mm (60–80 in)
- Elevation: 800–2,000m (2,600–6,500 ft)
- Soil pH: 5.5–6.5 (slightly acidic)
- Sunshine: ~1,800 hrs/year optimal
- Dry period: 2–3 months for fruit ripening

**Robusta** (30% of global production):

- Temperature: 22–30°C (72–86°F)
- Rainfall: 2,000–3,000mm (80–120 in)
- Elevation: 200–800m (650–2,600 ft)
- More heat- and disease-tolerant than Arabica

*Sources: Davis et al. (2012); ICO (2024).*

## Why geography matters: each country's story

**Brazil** — coffee grows across a 600–1,300m plateau with distinct wet and dry seasons. Only major producer growing both Arabica and Robusta commercially.

**Vietnam** — Central Highlands at 500–800m with volcanic basalt soils. Went from near-zero coffee production in the 1970s to the world's largest Robusta producer — one of the most dramatic agricultural transformations in history.

**Colombia** — three Andean mountain ranges, 1,200–2,000m, cloud cover, volcanic soil, **two rainy seasons per year**. Produces some of the world's most complex flavors (Federación Nacional de Cafeteros, 2023).

**Ethiopia** — the birthplace. Coffee still grows wild in highland forests at 1,500–2,200m. The only place coffee evolved naturally; its genetic diversity is irreplaceable for climate adaptation breeding (Davis et al., 2012).

**Indonesia** — volcanic island geography creates dramatically different flavor profiles from island to island — same species, radically different results.

## Climate change is rewriting this map

By **2050**, up to **50% of current coffee-growing land** will become unsuitable due to rising temperatures (Bunn et al., 2015). Recent signals:

- Brazil 2024: severe droughts + frosts → **70% price spike** in one year (Rabobank, 2024)
- Colombia 2023–2024: 13 months continuous excessive rain
- Ethiopia: 5-year ongoing drought

Adaptation strategies already in play — higher-elevation planting, shade systems, drought-resistant cultivars — are mostly driven by farmers, not policy.

## Key vocabulary

- **Coffee Belt** — equatorial commercial-coffee zone (25°N to 30°S)
- **Microclimate** — local climate differing from surroundings
- **Volcanic soil** — mineral-rich soil from volcanic activity
- **Terroir** — how geography, climate, and soil express themselves in flavor
- **Agroforestry** — growing coffee under shade tree canopy

# Welcome — The Morning Ritual

*Better Vice Club Episode 1 · Approximate listening time: 2 minutes*

Good morning. This is Anthony McDonald, and welcome to **Better Vice Club**.

Right now, someone somewhere is grinding coffee beans — as they have for centuries. The cup you'll make today connects you to **25 million farming families** across **70 countries**, a **$245 billion global industry** (International Coffee Organization, 2023), revolutions that toppled governments, and storytelling traditions older than the written word.

Over the next ~35 minutes (if you take the full episode) we'll follow coffee from the volcanic slopes where it grows to the economics of your local café, from the political revolutions it sparked to the stories hiding in every cup.

## What this course covers

- **Geography** — Where coffee grows, why, and what climate change means for your cup
- **Social Studies** — Coffeehouses, colonialism, and the ongoing work toward labor justice
- **Economics** — Who gets paid, how money flows through the coffee supply chain
- **English Language Arts** — Coffee in literature, language, advertising, and narrative

All four subject lenses align to Indiana Academic Standards and Common Core for grades 9–12. If you're a podcast listener, just enjoy — each segment is a self-contained story.

## How to use this course

- **Linear path:** go Lesson 1 → 2 → 3 → … in order. ~60–90 minutes total depending on depth.
- **Choose your own order (CYOA):** take Economics first, or Geography, or whatever grabs you. The lessons are designed to work standalone.
- **Teacher?** The Teacher Resources download on this lesson contains classroom activities, rubrics, knowledge-check answer keys, and IAS alignment notes.

## A note on the audio

This is a podcast-first course. Audio for each lesson drops according to the recording schedule. While audio is in production, every lesson has the full data sheet available as reading material, and the interactive maps/documents work independently. When audio publishes, it slots in alongside the text without disrupting your progress.

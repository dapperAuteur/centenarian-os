# Closing — Your Morning Altar

*Better Vice Club Episode 1 · Approximate listening time: 3 minutes*

We've covered a lot of ground — from Ethiopia's highland forests to London's coffeehouses, from commodity trading floors to the stories hiding in your neighborhood café.

Here's what I want to leave you with.

## Tomorrow morning

Before you take that first sip, take **ten seconds**. Think about:

- **Geography** — the elevation, the rainfall, the volcanic soil that made this possible
- **Social history** — the revolutions, the labor, the ongoing work toward justice
- **Economics** — where your money went, who benefits, what your purchase supports
- **Stories** — yours, your community's, the ones still waiting to be told

## The morning altar practice

I call this building a **morning altar** — not a religious thing, but a practice of intentional awareness. Your coffee isn't just caffeine. It's a daily connection to the planet, to history, to economic systems, and to the power of storytelling.

Most days the altar is quick — a single thought, a moment of gratitude, a question. Some days it's deeper. The point isn't ritualism; it's refusing to let your most-repeated daily action be invisible.

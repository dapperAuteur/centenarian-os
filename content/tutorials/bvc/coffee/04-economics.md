# Economics — Follow the Money

*Better Vice Club Episode 1 · Segment 3 · Approximate listening time: 8 minutes*

Coffee is the world's **second most traded agricultural commodity**. The global market was valued at **$245–269 billion in 2024**, with specialty coffee alone at **$101.6 billion** (SCA, 2024). Over **25 million farming families** produce coffee — roughly 100 million people dependent on this single crop.

## The market at a glance (2024)

- Total global market: **$245–269 B**
- Specialty coffee: **$101.6 B**
- Growth rate: 4.5–5.3% CAGR (2025–2030)
- Annual volume: 10.5–11.1 million tonnes green coffee

**Regional market share:**

- Europe: 34–36%
- North America: 28%
- Asia-Pacific: 25% (fastest growing, 6.2% CAGR)
- Other: 11%

*Sources: SCA (2024); ICO (2024).*

## Price volatility

Coffee is traded on the commodity C-Market. Historical swings:

- Average (2014–2024): **$1.42/lb**
- Low: $0.87/lb (2019)
- Recent high: **$3.20+/lb** (Nov 2024) — 27-year high, driven mostly by Brazil drought + frost (Rabobank, 2024)
- 2024 cumulative increase: **70% price surge**

**Volatility drivers:** Weather 60–70%, speculation 15–20%, currency 10–15%.

## The supply chain — who gets paid

> **[ILLUSTRATIVE — composite supply chain based on real industry data from ICO, Fair Trade USA, and SCA.]**

**Commodity system — $4.00 retail bag:**

| Stage | Price/lb | % of retail | Value added |
|---|---|---|---|
| Farm gate | $1.20 | 30% | Growing, harvesting, wet processing |
| Export | $1.45 | 6% | Quality grading, logistics |
| Import | $2.10 | 16% | Customs, warehousing, distribution |
| Roaster | $3.50 | 35% | Roasting, blending, packaging |
| Retail | $4.00 | 13% | Shelf space, marketing, sales |

**Specialty direct trade — $12.00 retail bag:**

| Stage | Price/lb | % of retail | Value added |
|---|---|---|---|
| Farm gate | $3.50–6.00 | 30–50% | Quality cultivation, relationships |
| Roaster | $8.50 | 20–40% | Small-batch, expertise |
| Retail | $12.00 | 10–30% | Market access, experience |

*Sources: Fair Trade USA (2023); ICO (2024); SCA (2024).*

Translation: in the commodity system, the farmer — the person doing the hardest, most climate-exposed, most skill-dependent work — captures roughly **30% of the retail price**. In direct trade, that jumps to **30–50%**, often at higher per-pound prices.

## Five economic concepts coffee teaches

| Concept | Definition | Coffee example |
|---|---|---|
| **Price elasticity** | How demand changes with price | Coffee demand is inelastic — 70% price increase, minimal consumption drop |
| **Value-added processing** | Transforming raw material to increase worth | Green beans → roasted → brewed |
| **Local multiplier effect** | Money recirculated locally | Local cafés recirculate **~68%** of revenue locally vs. **43%** for chains (AMIBA, 2023) |
| **Market segmentation** | Dividing market into distinct groups | Commodity (75%) vs. specialty (25%) |
| **Supply chain** | Network from production to consumption | Farm → export → import → roast → retail → cup |

## Fair Trade vs. Conventional vs. Direct Trade

| Factor | Conventional | Fair Trade | Direct Trade |
|---|---|---|---|
| Farm price/lb | $1.20 | $1.60 min + $0.20 premium | $3.50–6.00 |
| Price floor | None | Yes ($1.60) | Negotiated |
| Certification cost | None | $2,000–4,000/yr | None |
| Relationship length | Transaction | Annual | Multi-year |
| Quality requirement | Commodity grade | Minimum standard | High (80+ cup score) |

*Sources: Fair Trade USA (2023); SCA (2024).*

## What this means for your daily cup

When you buy coffee, you're not just buying caffeine. You're making a choice about **which economic system your money flows through**: commodity markets that prize volume, fair trade that guarantees floors, direct trade that compensates for quality. None of these are perfect. All are ongoing experiments in how to distribute value along a supply chain that includes farmers, roasters, baristas, and you.

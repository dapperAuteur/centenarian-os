## Narrator Script

Body composition metrics — weight, body fat percentage, muscle mass, and BMI — are locked by default in CentenarianOS. This lesson covers why that is, how to unlock them, and how to think about tracking this data constructively.

---

### Why Body Composition Is Locked

The lock on body composition metrics is intentional. Weight and body fat data can be triggering for people with a history of disordered eating or body image issues. The explicit acknowledgment before unlocking is a safeguard — a moment to confirm that you're choosing to track this data intentionally, not having it automatically thrust into your daily workflow.

If you don't want to track body composition, don't unlock it. The rest of the system works completely without it. The weekly review doesn't require it, the analytics don't require it, and nothing prompts you to unlock it.

If you do want to track it, the process is simple and the data can be genuinely useful.

---

### The Four Body Composition Metrics

**Weight (lbs)** — Body weight in pounds. Measured at the same time each day (usually morning, after using the bathroom, before eating) for the most consistent data. Daily weight fluctuates 1–3 lbs due to water, food, hormones — weekly averages are more meaningful than any single reading.

**Body Fat (%)** — The percentage of your weight that is fat mass. Measured via smart scale, DEXA scan, InBody scan, or calipers. Smart scales use bioelectrical impedance, which has meaningful error margins but is useful for tracking trends. DEXA and InBody are more accurate but less frequent.

**Muscle Mass (lbs)** — The total weight of muscle tissue. Increases with consistent resistance training and adequate protein intake. Decreasing muscle mass (sarcopenia) is one of the most critical factors in longevity — maintaining it as you age is the central goal.

**BMI** — Body Mass Index, calculated from height and weight. It's a rough population-level metric with well-known limitations (it can't distinguish between muscle and fat mass). Log it if your device or doctor tracks it, but use body fat percentage as the more meaningful individual indicator.

---

### Unlocking Body Composition Metrics

In the Body Composition section on the metrics page, you'll see four locked fields — each with its own lock icon and **Unlock** button. The unlock is per-metric, not all-or-nothing. You can unlock Weight but leave Body Fat locked, for example.

Click **Unlock** on any body composition field. A modal appears with a brief description of the metric and a more prominent acknowledgment statement about body image sensitivity. Click **Acknowledge & Unlock** to enable the field.

Unlike enrichment metrics, body composition unlocks have a slightly more deliberate acknowledgment flow — the modal is not dismissible by clicking outside it. You must click Acknowledge or Cancel. This is intentional.

---

### Logging Body Composition

Once unlocked, the body composition fields appear in the form below the enrichment metrics. Enter your values and save with the same **Save Today's Log** button.

For consistent results:
- **Weight**: Log first thing in the morning, before eating or drinking, after using the bathroom
- **Body Fat % and Muscle Mass**: Log when you have a reading from your scale or device. You don't need to log these daily — weekly or monthly readings are sufficient if your scale doesn't track them automatically
- **BMI**: Only log if your device or healthcare provider gives you a reading. There's little value in manually computing it

---

### Trends, Not Daily Numbers

The most important thing to understand about body composition data: single readings are noise. A weight reading 2 lbs higher than yesterday means nothing — water retention from a salty meal or a training session with high inflammation can cause that easily.

What matters is the trend over 4–12 weeks. The metrics page and analytics show you rolling averages precisely to smooth out the daily noise.

## Key Takeaways

- Body composition is locked by default — explicit per-metric acknowledgment required to unlock
- Four metrics: Weight (lbs), Body Fat (%), Muscle Mass (lbs), BMI
- Unlock is per-metric — unlock only what you want to track
- The acknowledgment modal is deliberate and can't be dismissed by clicking outside — confirms intentional choice
- Log weight first thing in the morning for consistency; body fat/muscle mass can be weekly or monthly
- Track trends over 4–12 weeks — daily fluctuations (1–3 lbs) are normal noise
- Muscle mass maintenance is a longevity priority — it's the most important of the four metrics

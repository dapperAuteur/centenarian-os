## Narrator Script

The Fuel dashboard is your daily landing page for the nutrition module. It surfaces the numbers that matter most — at a glance, without having to dig into any sub-page. In this final lesson, we'll walk through every section and explain what each metric is telling you.

---

### Navigating to the Dashboard

Go to `/dashboard/fuel`. This is the home screen for the entire Fuel module. If you've been logging meals and building protocols, you should start to see real data here.

---

### The Stats Cards

At the top of the dashboard, a row of summary cards shows your current activity snapshot:

**Meals logged** — Total meals recorded in your history. This number grows every time you log a home-cooked or restaurant meal. It's a simple accountability metric: are you actually tracking?

**Week cost** — Your estimated food cost for the current calendar week, based on the cost data from your protocols. Every logged protocol-based meal adds its cost-per-serving to this total. Restaurant meals don't contribute (no cost data). Use this as a rough gauge of your weekly food spend.

**Active batches** — How many meal prep batches currently have servings remaining. A quick answer to "do I have anything ready to eat?" If this is zero, it might be time to batch cook.

**Protocols** — The total number of protocols in your library. Not a performance metric — just a count of how many meal templates you've built.

---

### Low Stock Alerts

Below the stats cards (or alongside them), you'll see a **Low Stock** panel. Any ingredient at or below its threshold appears here with its current quantity.

This panel is meant to be your grocery list trigger. Before you close the app, glance at this section. If spinach is flagged, it goes on the shopping list. It's a simple feedback loop that keeps your inventory useful.

If all your ingredients are above threshold, the panel shows empty (or is hidden). That's the goal.

---

### Weekly NCV Average

If your analytics data is available, the dashboard shows your average NCV score for the current week. This is calculated from all logged protocol-based meals — each meal's NCV score is weighted by calories and averaged across the week.

A Green weekly average means most of your calories this week came from high-nutrient foods. Yellow means a mixed week. Red is a signal to look at what's pulling the score down.

This single number is the most useful output of the entire Fuel module. Track it week over week. A gradual trend toward Green is the goal — not perfection on any single day, but consistent progress over months.

---

### Navigation Cards

The lower section of the dashboard shows navigation cards linking to each sub-module. This is a visual menu — click any card to jump directly to Ingredients, Protocols, Meal Log, Inventory, Meal Prep, or Recipe Ideas.

For users who are just getting started, these cards double as orientation: they remind you what tools are available and suggest workflows.

---

### How the Pieces Fit Together: The Full Picture

After completing this course, you have the full picture of how the Fuel module works end to end:

1. **Ingredient Library** — Your food database. Every item you eat regularly is defined here with nutrition data and an NCV score.

2. **Protocols** — Your meal templates. Each meal you cook repeatedly has a protocol that lists its ingredients, calculates nutrition, and gives it an NCV score.

3. **Meal Log** — Your daily record. Protocol-based meals log in seconds; restaurant meals are noted with location.

4. **Inventory** — Tracks what you have on hand. Depletes automatically when you log meals. Alerts you when you're running low.

5. **Meal Prep** — Tracks batch cooking sessions. Tells you how many servings of prepared food you have left.

6. **Recipe Ideas** — AI-powered inspiration from your current inventory. Suggests what to cook based on what you have.

7. **Dashboard** — Pulls it all together: meals logged, weekly cost, active batches, low stock alerts, and NCV average.

The more consistently you log, the more useful the dashboard becomes. A week of consistent meal logging produces a NCV score you can actually act on. A month produces a trend.

## Key Takeaways

- **Meals logged** — your tracking consistency; **Week cost** — estimated food spend from protocol data
- **Active batches** — prepared food still available; **Protocols** — size of your meal template library
- **Low Stock panel** — your at-a-glance grocery list trigger; clear it before shopping
- **Weekly NCV average** — the most important metric: are you eating nutrient-dense food this week?
- Track NCV average week over week — a consistent Green trend is the long-term goal
- The dashboard is most useful when meal logging is consistent — one week of data is a snapshot; months of data is a trend

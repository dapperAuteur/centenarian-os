## Narrator Script

Some courses on the Academy use CYOA — Choose Your Own Adventure — navigation. Instead of a fixed lesson sequence, these courses offer you a choice at the end of each lesson: three different paths for where to go next. This lesson explains how it works and how to use it intentionally.

---

### What Is CYOA Mode?

CYOA mode is a navigation style where the course is a connected graph of lessons rather than a fixed sequence. At the end of each lesson, instead of one "Next" button, you see a **Choose Your Next Path** panel with three options.

Not all courses use CYOA. The course detail page shows the navigation mode. This course — the one you're in right now — uses CYOA.

---

### The Crossroads Panel

At the bottom of every lesson in CYOA mode, after the content, there's a crossroads panel. It shows three lesson options, each presented as a card with:

- **Lesson title**
- **Short description** — one sentence explaining what that lesson covers
- **Path type badge** — indicates how that lesson was chosen for you (see below)
- **Duration** — estimated reading/viewing time

Click any card to go to that lesson.

---

### The Three Path Types

The three options are generated by the **Crossroads API**, which uses a combination of three selection strategies:

**Linear** (in-sequence)
The lesson that comes next in the course's fixed sequence — the same lesson you'd see in linear mode. Useful when you want to follow the intended order.

*Badge appearance:* "Next Up" or a sequential icon.

**Semantic** (AI-recommended)
A lesson selected by AI (using vector embeddings of lesson content) as thematically related to what you just read. This is the discovery path — it may jump to a different module or a lesson you haven't seen yet that connects to the current topic.

*Badge appearance:* "Related" or a sparkle/AI icon.

**Random** (exploration)
A randomly selected unvisited lesson from the course. Useful for broad exploration when you don't want to follow any particular thread.

*Badge appearance:* "Explore" or a shuffle icon.

---

### How to Use CYOA Intentionally

**Follow Linear when:**
- You're new to the subject and want the structured progression the teacher designed
- You're near the end of the course and want to complete remaining lessons in order
- A lesson explicitly references "in the next lesson, we'll cover..."

**Follow Semantic when:**
- A concept in the current lesson sparked a question and you want to go deeper
- You want to explore connections the AI identified between topics
- You've already done a few linear lessons and want to branch out

**Follow Random when:**
- You've completed most of the course and want to fill in gaps without a particular goal
- You want to expose yourself to topics you might have skipped
- You're revisiting the course and want variety

---

### Progress in CYOA Mode

Progress works the same way as linear mode — Mark Complete still records completion and advances you. The difference is the "advance to next" behavior: in CYOA mode, instead of auto-advancing to a single next lesson, the crossroads panel appears.

If you Mark Complete and the crossroads panel appears, you can:
- Click one of the three path options to continue
- Close the crossroads panel and navigate via the sidebar to any lesson directly
- Leave the course and return later — your completed lessons are saved

---

### Completing a CYOA Course

A CYOA course is "complete" when you've marked every lesson done — regardless of the path order you took to get there. The progress percentage on My Courses reflects completed vs. total lessons.

Because CYOA courses are navigated non-linearly, you may reach lessons out of the "intended" order. This is by design. The crossroads panel will eventually surface all lessons as recommendations until you've covered everything.

## Key Takeaways

- CYOA mode presents three lesson choices at the end of each lesson instead of a fixed "Next"
- Three path types: Linear (next in sequence), Semantic (AI-recommended related lesson), Random (explore unvisited lessons)
- Use Linear for structure, Semantic for discovery, Random for exploration
- Progress works the same: Mark Complete saves completion; sidebar navigation still works
- A CYOA course is complete when all lessons are marked done, regardless of the order you took
- Not all courses use CYOA — check the course detail page for navigation mode

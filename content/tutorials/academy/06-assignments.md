## Narrator Script

Some courses include assignments — structured exercises where you apply what you've learned and submit your work for teacher review. This lesson covers how to find, complete, and submit assignments, and how to read the feedback you receive.

---

### What Are Assignments?

Assignments are optional or required exercises attached to a course. They appear as a separate tab or section on the course detail page (not as lessons in the lesson list). A teacher creates them and can attach specific data slots you need to fill in — called metric slots.

Not every course has assignments. The course detail page shows an **Assignments** tab only when the teacher has created at least one.

---

### Finding Assignments

From the course detail page, click the **Assignments** tab. This opens the assignment list for the course — titles, descriptions, and due dates if the teacher set them.

Click any assignment title to open the assignment viewer.

---

### The Assignment Viewer

The assignment viewer at `/academy/[courseId]/assignments/[assignmentId]` has two main areas:

**Left — Assignment Prompt**
The teacher's instructions: what you need to do, what to submit, and any reference materials. Read this carefully before starting.

**Metric Slots (if present)**
Some assignments include metric slots — structured input fields where you log specific data:
- A sleep hours observation ("Log your sleep hours for 7 consecutive days")
- An energy rating entry ("Rate your energy each morning for the next 5 days")
- A numeric measurement

Metric slots appear as labeled input fields within the prompt area. Fill them in as part of your submission.

**Right — Submission Area**
A text editor where you write your response — your reflection, analysis, answers to questions, or explanation of the data you logged in the metric slots.

The editor supports basic formatting. You can write as much or as little as the assignment requires.

---

### Saving a Draft

You don't have to submit the moment you start. Click **Save Draft** to store your in-progress response. Drafts are saved automatically every 30 seconds as well.

You can close the assignment and return later — your draft will be waiting. The assignment list shows a "Draft saved" indicator on assignments where you have an in-progress response.

---

### Submitting Your Assignment

When you're ready, click **Submit**. A confirmation dialog appears ("Submit this assignment? You won't be able to edit it after submission."). Click **Confirm** to finalize.

After submission:
- The submission area becomes read-only — you can still read your response but can't edit it
- The status badge on the assignment changes from "Draft" to "Submitted"
- The teacher is notified of your submission

---

### Teacher Feedback

After the teacher reviews your submission, they leave feedback as a threaded reply in the assignment viewer. You'll see a notification when feedback is available.

The feedback thread appears below your submission in the assignment viewer:
- Teacher's name and avatar
- Their feedback text
- Timestamp

If the teacher asks a follow-up question in their feedback, you can reply — the thread supports multiple back-and-forth exchanges. Click **Reply** below the feedback to add your response. Replies don't have the same "can't edit after submit" restriction — they work like a conversation.

---

### Assignment Status Labels

| Status | Meaning |
|--------|---------|
| Not started | You haven't opened or drafted anything yet |
| Draft | You've saved a draft but haven't submitted |
| Submitted | You submitted — awaiting teacher review |
| Feedback received | Teacher has replied — check the thread |

## Key Takeaways

- Assignments appear on the Assignments tab of the course detail page — not in the lesson list
- Assignment viewer has two areas: assignment prompt (with optional metric slots) and your submission text editor
- Save Draft saves your progress; drafts auto-save every 30 seconds
- After clicking Submit + Confirm, your submission is locked (read-only)
- Teacher feedback appears as a threaded reply below your submission — you can reply back
- Status labels: Not started → Draft → Submitted → Feedback received

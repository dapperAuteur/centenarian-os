## Narrator Script

Lessons can have threaded discussion sections where students and teachers can ask questions, share insights, and have conversations about the lesson content. This lesson covers how discussions work.

---

### Finding the Discussion Section

Scroll below the main lesson content (past the video/text/quiz area). If the lesson has discussions enabled, you'll see a **Discussion** section with a comment input field and any existing threads.

---

### Posting a Comment

Type your message in the input field and click **Post**. Your comment appears in the discussion thread with your name and a timestamp.

Comments are visible to all enrolled students and the course teacher. Be respectful and stay on topic — discussions are meant for lesson-related questions and insights.

---

### Threaded Replies

Each top-level comment can have replies. Click **Reply** below a comment to open a nested reply field. Post your reply to start a thread under that comment.

Threads are displayed as indented conversations below the parent comment. This keeps related discussion organized — a question and its answers stay grouped together.

---

### Teacher Responses

Teachers can see and respond to all discussion threads in their courses. Teacher responses are displayed with a visual indicator (often a highlighted name or badge) so you can identify official responses.

Discussion is a two-way channel — use it to ask questions you can't resolve from the lesson content alone.

---

### What Discussions Are Not

- **Not real-time chat** — discussions are asynchronous. Expect responses within hours or days, not seconds.
- **Not graded** — participation in discussions doesn't affect your progress or score.
- **Not on every lesson** — discussions are enabled per-lesson by the teacher. Some lessons may not have them.

## Key Takeaways

- Discussions appear below lesson content when enabled by the teacher
- Post comments and threaded replies for lesson-related questions and insights
- Teachers can respond to discussions — look for highlighted teacher names
- Discussions are asynchronous and not graded — they don't affect progress
- Not all lessons have discussions — it's an opt-in feature per lesson

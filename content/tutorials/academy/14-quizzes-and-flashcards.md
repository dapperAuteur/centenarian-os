## Narrator Script

Many courses test what you learn with quizzes, and back them up with flashcards for long-term recall. Both are built to make the facts stick, not just to grade you. This lesson covers how they work.

---

### Quiz lessons (rotating, spaced recall)

A quiz lesson appears in the curriculum like any other lesson. When you open one:

- **You get a fresh subset each time.** A quiz draws a random set of questions from a larger pool, and shuffles the answer choices, so retaking surfaces new questions instead of the same ones in the same order.
- **Attempts are unlimited, passing is 80%.** Retake as often as you want. Your best attempt stands.
- **Every question teaches.** After you answer, you see why the correct choice is right and why each other choice is wrong. The player gives extra weight to questions you've missed or haven't seen, so your weak spots come back around.

There's nothing to set up — just open the quiz and start.

---

### Key Terms lessons

Most chapters include a **Key Terms** lesson: the vocabulary for that chapter in one place. The full course glossary is collected in the **Resources** module, so you can study a chapter's terms as you go and review everything at the end.

---

### Flashcards (FlashLearn)

Some courses ship companion **flashcards** powered by FlashLearn AI for spaced repetition over days and weeks:

- **One deck per module**, in small sets (20 cards max) so a session is quick.
- **Two ways to study:** multiple-choice (pick the answer) or classic front/back recall.
- **Cards adapt to you.** Ones you miss reappear sooner; ones you know get spaced out. Your progress is tracked automatically — no separate account needed.

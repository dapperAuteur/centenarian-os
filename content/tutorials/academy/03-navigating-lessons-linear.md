# Lesson 03: Navigating Lessons (Linear Mode)

**Course:** Navigating the Centenarian Academy
**Module:** Learning Experience
**Duration:** ~5 min
**Lesson type:** text / video
**is_free_preview:** true
**CYOA navigation:** cyoa

---

## Narrator Script

Once you're inside a course, the lesson viewer is where you spend most of your time. This lesson covers linear mode — the step-by-step navigation that takes you through lessons in a fixed sequence. CYOA mode is covered in the next lesson.

---

### Opening the Lesson Viewer

From the course detail page, click any unlocked lesson title. The lesson viewer opens at `/academy/[courseId]/lessons/[lessonId]`.

---

### The Lesson Viewer Layout

The viewer has three areas:

**Left sidebar — Lesson List**
- Shows every lesson in the course in order
- Your current lesson is highlighted
- Completed lessons show a checkmark
- Locked lessons (unenrolled paid content) show a lock icon
- The sidebar is collapsible — click the arrow to expand it to full content view

**Center — Lesson Content**
- The main lesson content area: text, video embeds, or both depending on lesson type
- Text lessons render formatted markdown (headers, bold, bullet lists, code blocks)
- Video lessons embed the video player above the text content
- Content scrolls vertically — some lessons are long

**Bottom — Navigation Bar**
- **Previous** button (left) — goes to the prior lesson in sequence
- **Mark Complete** button (center) — marks this lesson done and advances automatically
- **Next** button (right) — advances without marking complete (useful for reviewing)
- Progress indicator: "Lesson X of Y" showing your position in the course

---

### Marking Lessons Complete

Click **Mark Complete** to record that you've finished the lesson. This:
- Adds a checkmark to the lesson in the sidebar
- Updates your course progress percentage
- Automatically advances to the next lesson

You can also click **Next** to move forward without marking complete — useful if you want to preview what's ahead without recording progress.

Marking a lesson complete is what drives the progress bar on My Courses and what determines whether a course shows as finished.

---

### Revisiting Lessons

All lessons you've already opened remain accessible — click any lesson in the sidebar to jump to it directly. There's no restriction on revisiting completed lessons.

If you marked a lesson complete but want to undo it, there's no un-mark button — but returning to a lesson doesn't subtract from your progress. Progress only increases.

---

### Free Preview Lessons in Linear Mode

If you open a course as a non-enrolled visitor using a preview lesson, the lesson viewer looks the same — but lessons not marked as free previews are locked. Clicking a locked lesson shows an enrollment prompt.

After enrolling, all locks lift immediately without needing to refresh.

---

### Progress Persistence

Progress saves automatically. Close the browser, switch devices, or log out — when you return to the course, the sidebar shows your completed lessons and the viewer resumes from where you left off (the first incomplete lesson).

## Key Takeaways

- Lesson viewer has three areas: left sidebar (lesson list + progress), center (content), bottom nav bar (Previous / Mark Complete / Next)
- Mark Complete = records progress + auto-advances; Next = advances without recording
- All completed lessons get a checkmark in the sidebar; progress only increases
- Jump to any lesson by clicking it in the sidebar — no restrictions on revisiting
- Progress saves automatically — pick up where you left off across sessions and devices
- Free preview lessons look identical to enrolled-mode lessons; locked lessons show an enrollment prompt

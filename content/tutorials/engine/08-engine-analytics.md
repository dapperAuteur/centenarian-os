## Narrator Script

The Engine Analytics page turns your session history into patterns. It's not a vanity dashboard — it's a diagnostic tool. The data it shows is only useful if you look at it regularly and act on what you find.

---

### Navigating to Analytics

From the Engine dashboard or navigation, click **Analytics**. The analytics page has four tabs: **Overview**, **Trends**, **Pomodoro**, and **Performance**.

---

### Overview Tab

The Overview tab shows your focus metrics for a selected time period (default: last 30 days). Key stats:

**Total focus minutes** — Total logged focus time in the period. This is the headline metric. If your goal is 20 hours of deep work per week and you logged 12, the gap is visible here.

**Sessions completed** — Count of focus sessions in the period.

**Average session duration** — Mean session length. Useful for calibrating your templates and goals. If your average is 28 minutes but you always set 45-minute sessions, you're regularly stopping early.

**Average quality rating** — Mean quality across all sessions. Below 3.0 consistently means something is interfering with your focus — worth investigating. Above 4.0 consistently means you're creating the right conditions for deep work.

**Focus minutes by tag** — A breakdown of your focused time by task category (FITNESS, CREATIVE, SKILL, OUTREACH, etc., matching the tags in your Planner). This shows where your focused time is actually going relative to your goals. If 80% of your focus time is on OUTREACH but CREATIVE is the goal you care most about, the chart surfaces that imbalance.

**Sessions per day of week** — Which days do you run the most sessions? Most people see a clear weekly pattern. If you're aiming for focus on Wednesdays but the chart shows your lowest session count on Wednesdays, something about your Wednesday schedule is working against you.

---

### Trends Tab

The Trends tab shows how your focus metrics have changed over time. The default view shows a 12-week rolling window.

**Weekly focus minutes** — Bar chart showing total focus minutes per week. Spot trends: are you building momentum? Is there a consistent dip in certain weeks? Did a change in your environment or schedule affect your focus output?

**Quality over time** — Line chart showing your average quality rating per week. This is the most important trend to watch. A declining quality trend with stable or rising volume means you're logging more sessions but getting less out of them — often a sign of fatigue or increasing distraction.

**Session streak** — Days with at least one focus session. Streaks are useful for building the habit early, but long-term, consistency (most days, most weeks) matters more than unbroken streaks.

**Energy correlation** — If you've been logging daily debriefs with energy ratings, this chart overlays your average daily energy with your session quality rating. The correlation (or lack of it) tells you how much your physical energy predicts your focus quality.

---

### Pomodoro Tab

The Pomodoro tab shows analytics specific to Pomodoro-mode sessions.

**Pomodoro sessions vs. Simple sessions** — Proportion of each mode in your history. Useful if you're intentionally shifting toward more Pomodoro or more Simple usage.

**Average cycles per session** — For Pomodoro sessions, how many work intervals do you typically complete? If you consistently stop at 2 of 4 planned cycles, you may be over-estimating your available time or under-estimating the task.

**Break skip rate** — What percentage of scheduled breaks do you skip? A high skip rate might mean your work intervals are too short (you're in flow when the break triggers) or that you feel guilty about breaks and skip them reflexively.

**Most-used interval configuration** — Your most common work/break combination. This helps you decide whether your default Pomodoro template is actually the one you use most.

---

### Performance Tab

The Performance tab connects your focus data to your plan outcomes.

**Task completion vs. focus minutes** — Correlation between the weeks you logged the most focus time and the weeks you completed the most tasks. High correlation means your focus sessions are converting to plan execution. Low correlation means you may be logging sessions for low-value tasks.

**Focus minutes by milestone** — A breakdown of total focused time by milestone. This shows which milestones are receiving the most focused attention. If a high-priority milestone shows almost no session time, either the tasks aren't being linked to sessions or the work isn't happening.

**Session revenue / cost** — If you've logged financial data on sessions, this tab shows total session revenue, total session cost, and net output for the period.

---

### Using Analytics to Improve

The most common pattern improvement cycles:

1. **Low total minutes** → Review your schedule. Are you protecting focus time? Check whether recurring tasks in the Planner are blocking time for focused work.

2. **Low quality average** → Investigate your environment. Time of day, noise, notification load, physical energy (check energy ratings from daily debrief).

3. **Quality declining over time** → Often a training load / recovery issue. Check pain logs and health metrics from the same period.

4. **Focus minutes not matching plan priorities** → Check the "by milestone" breakdown. Reprioritize what you start sessions for.

## Key Takeaways

- Four analytics tabs: Overview (summary stats), Trends (time-series patterns), Pomodoro (Pomodoro-specific metrics), Performance (plan execution correlation)
- Overview: total minutes, sessions, average duration, average quality, minutes by tag, sessions by day of week
- Trends: weekly minutes chart, quality over time, energy/quality correlation (if debriefs logged)
- Pomodoro tab: cycles per session, break skip rate, most-used interval config
- Performance tab: focus minutes by milestone — shows where your deep work time is actually going
- Most useful diagnostic: declining quality with stable or rising volume = fatigue signal
- Check analytics weekly, not daily — weekly patterns reveal what daily numbers hide

## Narrator Script

Most productivity systems track what you did. The Engine tracks how you worked — the quality of your focus, the state of your body, and the relationship between the two over time.

It's built around a simple insight: sustainable performance requires monitoring both output and recovery. Logging that you completed 3 hours of deep work is useful. Knowing that those 3 hours happened on a day when your energy was 4/5 and you had no pain — and that your worst focus days correlate with poor sleep — is something you can actually act on.

---

### What the Engine Is

The Engine is CentenarianOS's focus and recovery module. It has five core sections:

**Focus Sessions** — timed work blocks. You log what you did, link it to a task in your plan, and rate the quality of your focus at the end. Sessions can run in Simple mode (pure timer) or Pomodoro mode (alternating work and break intervals).

**Session Templates** — saved session presets. If you run the same type of session regularly (a 45-minute writing block, a 25/5 Pomodoro, a 2-hour deep coding session), templates let you start them with one click instead of configuring from scratch each time.

**Daily Debrief** — a short end-of-day check-in. You rate your energy for the day, write your biggest win, write your biggest challenge, and log any financial data for the day. One entry per day — it takes about 2 minutes.

**Pain Log** — body state tracking. Log any pain or discomfort: where it is, how intense, what type of sensation, and what activity may have caused it. This data connects to the recovery sections of the weekly review.

**Session History** — every focus session you've run, with filters, edit capability, and export. Useful for reviewing your work patterns or correcting a session entry.

### The Engine Dashboard

Navigate to `/dashboard/engine`. You'll see a stats grid at the top showing:

- **Today's focus minutes** — total time logged in focus sessions today
- **This week's focus minutes** — total for the current week
- **Sessions this week** — count of sessions, not just time
- **Daily goal progress** — how close you are to your daily focus goal (set in Lesson 09)

Below the stats grid, you'll see:

- **Recent sessions** — the last few focus sessions you've run with time, activity, and quality rating
- **Start a Focus Session** button — the primary action
- **Daily Debrief** section — today's debrief (or a prompt to complete one if not done)

The Engine is designed to be your daily entry point for the work side of CentenarianOS. Open it, start a session, close the session with a rating, and move on. The data accumulates silently.

---

### Why Quality Ratings Matter

At the end of every focus session, you rate the quality of your focus on a scale of 1 to 5. This is the most important data point the Engine collects.

It's a subjective rating — 5 means you were deeply focused, in flow, highly productive. 1 means you were constantly distracted, couldn't concentrate, barely made progress.

Over time, your quality ratings tell you things your calendar can't. A week of 4s and 5s followed by a drop to 2s and 3s is a pattern worth investigating. That pattern often correlates with something trackable — sleep quality from your health logs, nutrition changes in your Fuel data, or increased task load from the Planner.

You can't optimize what you don't measure.

---

### How the Engine Connects to the Rest of the System

The Engine doesn't operate in isolation. Every focus session can be linked to a specific task in your Planner — so your deep work time contributes to task completion. Your session quality data feeds into the weekly review that Gemini generates. Your pain logs connect to the recovery analysis in the review.

The more consistently you use the Engine, the more the weekly review reflects the full picture of how you're performing — not just what you completed, but how hard it was to get there.

## Key Takeaways

- The Engine has five sections: Focus Sessions, Session Templates, Daily Debrief, Pain Log, Session History
- Dashboard shows: today's focus minutes, weekly total, session count, daily goal progress
- Two timer modes: Simple (pure countdown/countup) and Pomodoro (work + break intervals)
- Quality rating (1–5) at session end is the most actionable data point the Engine collects
- Engine data feeds into the AI weekly review — energy, pain, and focus quality are all included
- Primary daily workflow: start session → work → close with quality rating → done

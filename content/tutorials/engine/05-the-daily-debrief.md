## Narrator Script

The daily debrief is a 2-minute end-of-day check-in that captures three things: how your energy felt, what went well, and what didn't. It takes almost no time, but over weeks it creates a longitudinal record of your daily state that you can't get from task completion data alone.

---

### What the Daily Debrief Captures

One debrief per day. It has four fields:

**Energy rating** — How was your energy level for this day, overall? Rate 1 to 5.
- **5** — High energy all day. Alert, motivated, physically capable.
- **4** — Good energy. Some dips but mostly strong.
- **3** — Average. A few productive hours, some fatigue.
- **2** — Low energy. Hard to concentrate, tired, sluggish.
- **1** — Exhausted. Minimal productive capacity.

This is the single most useful field in the daily debrief. It correlates with every other variable you track — sleep quality, nutrition, training load, pain levels.

**Biggest win** — One thing that went well today. It doesn't have to be spectacular. "Shipped a feature I'd been putting off" is as valid as "Completed a personal record in the gym." The habit of identifying one positive outcome per day is worth more than the specific content.

**Biggest challenge** — One thing that was hard or didn't go well. A task that took three times longer than expected, a conversation that was difficult, a training session that fell apart, a meal plan that broke down. Write what actually happened, not a sanitized version.

**Daily finances** (optional) — Revenue and expenses for the day. These are different from the financial fields on tasks in the Planner — they're for ad hoc financial activity that doesn't belong to a specific task. Day-trading activity, a freelance invoice, an unexpected expense.

---

### Where to Log the Debrief

The debrief section is visible on the Engine dashboard. If you haven't completed today's debrief, you'll see a prompt with a **Log Today's Debrief** button.

Click it. A modal opens with the four fields. Fill them in and click **Save**.

You can also find the debrief in the Engine daily log section, accessible from the Engine navigation.

---

### Editing a Debrief

If you want to add to or correct a debrief entry, click **Edit** on the logged debrief. All four fields are editable. Changes are saved immediately.

You can only have one debrief per calendar day — if you click "Log Today's Debrief" and one already exists for today, the edit form opens instead of creating a duplicate.

---

### How Debrief Data Appears in the Weekly Review

Your energy ratings for the week are averaged and included in the weekly review. A week where your average energy was 2.1 looks very different from one where it was 4.3 — and the review will reference it directly.

The biggest wins and challenges are not summarized by Gemini (they're qualitative text), but they're stored in your history. Reading through a week's wins and challenges in the weekly review debrief section gives you a narrative of how the week felt, not just what the numbers show.

---

### The Best Time to Log It

End of the work day — before you switch into personal mode. Or in the evening before bed. Not in the morning about the previous day, if you can avoid it — recency bias means today's mood will color your rating of yesterday.

The debrief takes under 2 minutes. If it's taking longer than that, you're overthinking it. Write what comes to mind first. The first answer is usually the most honest.

## Key Takeaways

- One debrief per day: energy rating (1–5), biggest win, biggest challenge, optional daily finances
- Energy rating is the most valuable field — tracks correlation with sleep, nutrition, pain, and training load
- Log end-of-day or evening — not the next morning
- Takes under 2 minutes — don't overthink it, write the first honest answer
- Energy averages for the week appear in the AI weekly review
- Qualitative wins and challenges are stored in history — useful for reading week-over-week narratives

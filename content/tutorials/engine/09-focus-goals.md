## Narrator Script

Focus goals give the Engine a target to measure against. Without a goal, your focus minutes are just a number. With a goal, they're a percentage of what you intended — and the dashboard shows you exactly where you stand.

---

### What Focus Goals Are

Focus goals are daily and weekly minute targets for focused work. They live in your Engine settings and power the progress indicators on the Engine dashboard.

**Daily focus goal** — How many minutes of focused work you aim to log each day. A common starting point for knowledge workers: 120 minutes (2 hours of deep work). Athletes doing double days might log focused study or creative work in addition to training — 60–90 minutes may be more realistic. Adjust to what's achievable on a typical day, not your best day.

**Weekly focus goal** — Total focused minutes for the week. You can set this independently from the daily goal (e.g., you might have a 120-minute daily goal but a 600-minute weekly goal if you only plan to focus 5 days). Or set it as a simple multiple (daily × 5).

---

### Setting Your Focus Goals

From the Engine dashboard, click **Settings** (gear icon) or navigate to **Engine Settings**.

The settings panel shows:

**Daily focus goal** — Input field in minutes. Enter your target.

**Weekly focus goal** — Input field in minutes.

Click **Save**. Goals take effect immediately — the dashboard progress indicators update.

---

### The Dashboard Progress Indicators

Once goals are set, the Engine dashboard stats grid shows progress bars under the focus minute counts:

**Today's focus minutes / Daily goal** — A horizontal progress bar. If your daily goal is 120 minutes and you've logged 85, the bar is at 71%. The color changes as you approach and hit the goal: neutral below 50%, amber at 50–85%, green at 85%+.

**This week's focus minutes / Weekly goal** — Same progress bar structure for the week total.

These progress bars are the most visible part of the Engine dashboard. They tell you at a glance whether today's focused work is on track, without requiring you to calculate anything.

---

### Calibrating Your Goals

Start conservative. A goal you consistently hit builds the habit; a goal you consistently miss erodes motivation.

If you're new to the Engine: set your daily goal to 60 minutes. After two weeks, look at your session history average. If you're consistently exceeding 60 minutes, raise the goal to 90. Build from there.

If you're an experienced user: set the goal to 80% of your best recent week. That gives you a challenging but realistic target that accounts for bad days.

Review your goals quarterly. A goal that was right in February may be wrong in August — your life, workload, and energy fluctuate.

---

### Focus Goals vs. Task Deadlines

Focus goals measure time input. Task completion in the Planner measures output. Both matter. Neither replaces the other.

A week where you hit your focus goal but complete very few tasks might mean your session quality is low (check the quality average) or that you're spending focus time on low-priority tasks (check the Performance analytics tab).

A week where task completion is high but focus minutes are low might mean you're completing tasks through quick bursts of execution rather than deep work — which is sometimes fine, but often not sustainable.

Use both metrics together, not either/or.

## Key Takeaways

- Focus goals set daily and weekly minute targets for the Engine dashboard progress bars
- Set in Engine Settings: Daily focus goal (minutes) + Weekly focus goal (minutes)
- Dashboard shows progress bars: today vs. daily goal, this week vs. weekly goal
- Color coding: neutral below 50%, amber 50–85%, green 85%+
- Start conservative — a goal you consistently hit is more valuable than one you consistently miss
- Review goals quarterly — they should reflect your current life and workload, not your ideal
- Focus goals (time input) and task completion (output) are complementary — use both

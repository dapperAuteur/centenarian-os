## Narrator Script

You track transactions in Finance, trips in Travel, workouts in Workouts, and tasks in Planner. Each module has its own internal categories — budget categories for transactions, equipment categories for gear. But what if you want to see everything related to "Health" in one place, regardless of which module it lives in? That's what Life Categories are for.

---

### The Problem: Siloed Categories

Each module organizes data its own way:

| Module | Internal Categories |
|--------|-------------------|
| Finance | Budget categories (Groceries, Rent, Gas, etc.) |
| Equipment | Equipment categories (Electronics, Furniture, etc.) |
| Travel | Trip types (commute, business, fitness, etc.) |
| Workouts | Exercise types |

These work great within their modules. But they can't answer cross-module questions like:
- "How much do I spend on **Health** across gym memberships, supplements, and medical visits?"
- "What **Career** activities did I do this month — courses, work trips, invoices?"
- "Show me everything related to my **Home** — maintenance, equipment purchases, cleaning tasks."

---

### The Solution: Life Categories

Life Categories are a tagging layer that sits on top of every module. They let you assign high-level life areas to any item:

| Category | Color | Example Items |
|----------|-------|---------------|
| Health | Red | Doctor appointment (task), gym membership (transaction), morning run (workout) |
| Finance | Green | Tax prep (task), invoice payment (transaction), budgeting session (focus) |
| Career | Blue | Client meeting (task), business trip (trip), freelance invoice (invoice) |
| Home | Amber | Lawn mowing (task), furniture purchase (equipment), plumber visit (maintenance) |
| Fitness | Pink | Gym session (workout), protein powder (transaction), running shoes (equipment) |
| Travel | Cyan | Road trip (trip), travel gear (equipment), hotel booking (transaction) |
| Learning | Violet | Online course (task), textbook (transaction), study session (focus) |
| Social | Orange | Dinner with friends (transaction), group hike (trip), party planning (task) |

---

### How They Work

- **Any entity can be tagged** — tasks, trips, transactions, workouts, equipment, invoices, recipes, fuel logs, maintenance records, routes, and focus sessions
- **Multiple tags per item** — a gym membership transaction could be tagged both "Health" and "Fitness"
- **Tags are personal** — your categories are yours; they don't affect the underlying data
- **Auto-seeded defaults** — 8 categories are created automatically the first time you access the feature
- **Fully customizable** — rename, recolor, reorder, delete, or create new categories

---

### Where to Find Them

Life Categories appear in two places:

1. **Edit modals** — below Activity Links on every entity edit modal (tasks, workouts, invoices, trips, etc.)
2. **Categories dashboard** — `/dashboard/categories` in the Life nav group

## Key Takeaways

- Life Categories are cross-module tags — they work across all 11 entity types
- They answer questions that module-specific categories can't (e.g., "all Health spending")
- 8 defaults are auto-created: Health, Finance, Career, Home, Fitness, Travel, Learning, Social
- Items can have multiple life categories
- Find them in entity edit modals and the Categories dashboard

## Narrator Script

Once you've tagged a good portion of your data, the Categories dashboard becomes a powerful lens for understanding your life. This lesson covers the analytical features — how to read the charts, spot trends, and use the data to make better decisions.

---

### Spending Breakdown

The pie chart on the Categories dashboard aggregates all tagged financial transactions:

- **Expenses** show as negative amounts per category
- **Income** shows as positive amounts
- **Net** is calculated per category

This tells you at a glance how your money flows across life areas. If "Health" is taking 40% of your spending, you can see that immediately — even though the underlying transactions are spread across groceries, gym memberships, supplements, and medical bills in the Finance module.

---

### Activity Distribution

The bar chart shows entity counts per category. A tall "Career" bar with a lot of task segments means you're spending most of your logged activity time on work. A tall "Fitness" bar with workout and trip segments means you're active and tracking it well.

Use this to spot imbalances. If "Social" has zero items for the last 30 days, that might be worth noticing.

---

### Combining with Module Analytics

Life Categories don't replace module-specific analytics — they complement them:

| Question | Where to Look |
|----------|--------------|
| "How much did I spend on Health?" | Categories dashboard — pie chart |
| "What were the specific Health transactions?" | Finance dashboard — filter by budget category |
| "How many Health-related workouts vs trips?" | Categories dashboard — bar chart segments |
| "What's my MPG trend?" | Travel dashboard — fuel analytics |
| "Which life areas am I most active in?" | Categories dashboard — summary cards |

---

### Tips for Better Analytics

1. **Tag consistently** — the analytics are only as good as your tagging. Make it a habit to tag items as you create them.
2. **Use the uncategorized view weekly** — a quick 5-minute session to tag anything you missed keeps your data clean.
3. **Adjust the period** — compare 7-day vs 30-day vs 90-day views to spot short-term vs long-term trends.
4. **Create specific categories** — if "Health" is too broad, create sub-categories like "Medical", "Nutrition", "Gym" for more granular analysis.
5. **Review after imports** — bulk-imported data won't have life category tags. The uncategorized view makes catching up fast.

---

### Cross-Module Questions You Can Answer

With life categories in place, you can now answer questions that were impossible before:

- "How much of my time and money goes to Fitness each month?"
- "What percentage of my spending is Career-related?"
- "Am I investing more in Learning or Social activities this quarter?"
- "Which life area generates the most activity links?"
- "How does my Home spending compare to my Travel spending?"

These are the kinds of high-level life questions that CentenarianOS was built to answer — and Life Categories make them visible.

## Key Takeaways

- The spending pie chart aggregates all tagged financial transactions by life area
- The activity bar chart shows entity counts with entity type breakdowns
- Life Categories complement module-specific analytics — use both together
- Tag consistently and use the uncategorized view weekly for clean data
- Adjust the period selector to compare short-term vs long-term trends
- Create specific categories if the defaults are too broad for your needs

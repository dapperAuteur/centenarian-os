## Narrator Script

You've logged a few fill-ups. Now let's talk about what all that data is telling you.

The Fuel dashboard takes your raw log entries and surfaces them as trends and summaries. This is where the data becomes insight — not just "I paid $44 for gas" but "my MPG has dropped 3 miles per gallon over the last two months."

Let's walk through everything you'll see on this page.

---

### The Fuel Dashboard Overview

Navigate to `/dashboard/travel/fuel`. At the top, you'll see summary cards — a quick snapshot of your current fuel performance.

**Average MPG** — Your fuel efficiency averaged across all logged fill-ups for the selected vehicle. This is a rolling average, so it updates with every new entry.

**Total fuel spend** — How much you've spent on gas in the current month, or over your selected date range. Useful for budgeting.

**Cost per mile** — Total fuel spend divided by total miles driven. This is one of the most useful numbers in the module — it lets you compare true driving cost against, say, the cost of biking or public transit.

**Total miles tracked** — The cumulative miles logged across all your fill-ups.

---

### The MPG Trend Chart

Below the summary cards, you'll see a line chart showing your MPG over time. Each data point represents one fill-up.

What to look for:

**Steady flatline** — Your vehicle is performing consistently. Normal.

**Gradual downward trend** — MPG creeping down over weeks or months often signals that maintenance is overdue: dirty air filter, degraded spark plugs, or tires that need air.

**Sharp one-time drop** — A single outlier usually means a data entry error. Check that fill-up's odometer reading — you may have entered a wrong digit.

**Seasonal dips** — In cold climates, MPG naturally drops in winter. Cold air is denser, engines take longer to warm up, and road conditions increase rolling resistance. Don't panic over a winter dip.

---

### The Cost Trend Chart

Below or alongside the MPG chart, you'll see fuel cost tracked over time — either cost per fill-up or cost per month, depending on your view.

This chart is useful for noticing when gas prices in your area spike, and for projecting monthly fuel budgets.

---

### Filtering by Vehicle and Date Range

If you have multiple vehicles, use the vehicle selector at the top of the dashboard to switch between them. Each vehicle has its own independent data.

The date range filter lets you zoom in on a specific period. Want to see MPG for the last 90 days vs. the whole year? Adjust the range.

---

### The Fuel Log Table

Below the charts is the full log table — every fill-up, in reverse chronological order. Each row shows:
- Date
- Odometer at fill-up
- Gallons
- Price per gallon
- Total cost
- MPG for that fill-up

You can click **Edit** on any row to correct a mistake, or **Delete** to remove an entry entirely.

---

### Using the Data

Here's a practical way to use this dashboard every month:

1. Check your average MPG — is it holding steady or trending down?
2. Check your monthly fuel spend — does it match what you expected?
3. Look for outlier MPG entries — fix data errors before they distort the trend
4. Compare cost per mile against your bike savings (we'll cover that in the Trips section)

A few minutes reviewing this data monthly is enough to catch maintenance issues before they become expensive repairs.

## Key Takeaways

- Summary cards show average MPG, total spend, cost per mile, and total miles at a glance
- The MPG trend chart reveals maintenance issues before they become serious
- Sharp single-point MPG drops usually mean a data entry error — check the odometer
- Seasonal MPG dips in winter are normal in cold climates
- Filter by vehicle and date range to isolate specific periods for analysis

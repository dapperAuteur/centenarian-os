## Narrator Script

Before you can log a single fuel fill-up or maintenance record, you need to create a vehicle profile. This is the anchor for all your travel data — every trip, every oil change, every gallon of gas is linked to a specific vehicle.

In this lesson, we'll add your first vehicle from scratch. I'll show you every field in the form, which ones matter most, and how to edit or delete a vehicle later.

---

### Opening the Vehicle Setup

Head to your Travel dashboard at `/dashboard/travel`. At the top of the page, you'll see your vehicles listed — or, if this is your first time, a prompt to add one.

Click the **Add Vehicle** button. This opens the vehicle creation form.

---

### Filling In the Vehicle Form

The form has several fields. Let me walk you through each one.

**Make** — The manufacturer. For example: Toyota, Honda, Ford, Subaru. Type it in — it's a free text field, not a dropdown.

**Model** — The specific model. For example: Camry, Civic, F-150, Outback.

**Year** — The model year. Four digits, like 2019.

**Fuel type** — You'll select from a dropdown: Gasoline, Diesel, Electric, or Hybrid. This affects how the MPG calculations are labeled in your fuel logs.

**Current odometer** — This is important. Enter the current mileage on your vehicle's odometer right now. This establishes your baseline — future fuel logs and maintenance records will reference this number. If you're not sure, go check your dashboard and come back. It's worth getting right.

**Nickname** (optional) — If you have multiple vehicles, a nickname helps you tell them apart quickly. For example: "Daily Driver", "Road Trip Truck", "My Bike" (yes, you can add bikes too, even though they don't take fuel).

---

### Saving the Vehicle

Once you've filled in all the required fields — Make, Model, Year, Fuel Type, and Odometer — click **Save**. The vehicle card will appear on your Travel dashboard.

Each vehicle card shows you a summary: the make, model, year, and current mileage. As you add fuel logs, the mileage will update automatically based on your entries.

---

### Adding Multiple Vehicles

You can have as many vehicles as you need. Click **Add Vehicle** again to add a second one. If you have a car and a bike, add both. Each one has its own fuel logs, trips, and maintenance records — completely separate.

---

### Editing or Deleting a Vehicle

If you made a typo or need to update the odometer baseline, click the **Edit** button on the vehicle card. The same form opens with your current values pre-filled. Make your changes and save.

To delete a vehicle, click **Delete** on the card. You'll see a confirmation prompt — this is permanent, so be sure before you confirm. Deleting a vehicle removes all its associated fuel logs and maintenance records.

---

### Rental and Borrowed Vehicles

Not every vehicle you track is one you own. Maybe you rented a car for a road trip, borrowed a friend's truck to move furniture, or picked up a moving van for a week. You still want to log the fuel and trips — but you don't want that data mixed into your personal savings stats.

When you add a vehicle, the form has an **Ownership type** field:
- **Owned** (default) — Your vehicle. Its fuel spend counts toward your cost-per-mile calculation and bike savings comparison.
- **Rental** — A temporary vehicle you're paying for. Fuel is logged normally, but its costs are excluded from your personal averages.
- **Borrowed** — Someone else's vehicle. Same exclusion as rental.

Rental and borrowed vehicles show an amber badge on their card so you can tell them apart at a glance. You can still log fuel fill-ups and trips against a rental — it's useful to have the record — but the numbers won't inflate your personal car cost per mile.

When a rental period ends and you no longer need the vehicle in your list, you can retire it (more on that below).

---

### Retiring a Vehicle

If you sell a car, donate a bike, or simply stop using a vehicle, you don't have to delete it and lose all your data. Instead, you can **retire** it.

Click the **Retire** button on the vehicle card. You'll be asked to confirm. Once retired:
- The vehicle moves to a "Retired vehicles" section below your active fleet
- Its card is shown in grayscale with a "Retired" badge
- All fuel logs, trips, and maintenance records tied to it are fully preserved
- The vehicle no longer appears in dropdowns when logging new fuel, trips, or maintenance entries

If you ever need to bring a vehicle back — say you sold a car but got it back, or reactivated a seasonal bike — click **Reactivate** on the retired card. It returns to your active fleet immediately.

The retired section is collapsed by default, so it stays out of the way. Click "Show retired vehicles" to expand it.

---

### After This Lesson: Your First Crossroads

Now that your vehicle is set up, you're ready to explore the module. After you mark this lesson complete, you'll see a branching menu — your first Choose Your Own Adventure moment in this course.

From there, you can go in any direction:
- **Fuel Logs** — Learn to log fill-ups and use the OCR receipt scanner
- **Trips & Activities** — Log trips manually or import from Garmin
- **Vehicle Maintenance** — Track service records and maintenance history

There's no wrong order. Most people start with Fuel Logs since that's the most frequent activity, but jump wherever makes the most sense for how you plan to use the module.

## Key Takeaways

- Every vehicle needs: Make, Model, Year, Fuel Type, and current Odometer reading
- The odometer baseline is critical — it's used to calculate MPG and track mileage over time
- You can add multiple vehicles (cars, bikes, trucks — anything)
- **Ownership type** distinguishes your personal vehicles from rentals/borrowed — rental data is excluded from your personal savings stats
- **Retire** a vehicle to preserve its history without cluttering your active fleet; **Reactivate** it any time
- Edit or delete vehicles from the vehicle card on the Travel dashboard
- After this lesson, you'll choose which topic to explore next: Fuel Logs, Trips, or Maintenance

## Narrator Script

Welcome to Mastering Travel Tracking — a free tutorial course for CentenarianOS.

Whether you drive a car, ride a bike, or mix both, this module is designed to turn your daily movement into meaningful data. By the time you finish this course, you'll know how to track every fuel fill-up, log trips, stay on top of vehicle maintenance, and even pull in activity data directly from your Garmin device.

Let's start with a quick tour of the Travel dashboard so you know where everything lives.

---

### What is the Travel Module?

The Travel module is one of CentenarianOS's data-tracking tools, sitting inside your main dashboard. It has four core areas:

**Fuel Logs** — Every time you fill up your tank, you can log the cost, gallons, and odometer reading. Over time, you get automatic MPG calculations and cost trend charts. If you hate typing numbers, you can photograph your receipt and let the AI extract the data for you.

**Trips** — A trip is any journey you want to remember or analyze: a road trip, a daily commute, a bike ride to work. You can log trips manually or import them directly from Garmin. The module tracks distance by mode of transport — car miles vs. bike miles — so you can see how much money you're saving by cycling instead of driving.

**Vehicle Maintenance** — Oil changes, tire rotations, brake jobs — everything logged with date, cost, and mileage. Over time, this gives you a full service history for every vehicle you own.

**Import** — If you use a Garmin device, you can export your activity files and upload them here. Cycling, walking, running, and hiking activities are automatically converted into trips. Gym workouts are skipped, since those don't involve travel.

---

### Why Track This Data?

Most people have no idea what their vehicle actually costs them per mile. They know the sticker price of gas, but they don't factor in how many miles per gallon they're getting, how that's trending over time, or what maintenance is adding up to.

CentenarianOS gives you that full picture. And when you pair vehicle costs with bike ride data, you start to see real dollar savings from choosing two wheels over four.

This course will teach you every feature in the module, step by step. You'll be up and running in under an hour.

After that, you'll have four topic areas to explore in any order you choose: Fuel Logs, Trips & Activities, and Vehicle Maintenance. The course is designed with branching navigation, so once you finish Getting Started, you can jump to whatever topic interests you most.

## Narrator Script

One of the most useful features in the Coach is something that happens automatically in the background: flashcard generation. When a Gem is designed to include flashcards in its responses, those cards are extracted and saved to your flashcard library without any action from you. This lesson covers how to set it up and what it's best used for.

---

### What Flashcard Generation Does

When the AI generates a response, the system scans the response for a specific syntax block. If it finds one, it automatically creates a flashcard set from the cards inside. This happens asynchronously — it doesn't delay the chat response you see, and you don't have to trigger it manually.

The flashcards are saved to your account and available for review in the Flashcards section of the Academy.

---

### The Syntax

To trigger flashcard creation, the AI's response must contain:

```
[START_FLASHCARDS]
F::front of the card
B::back of the card
F::another question or concept
B::the answer or definition
[END_FLASHCARDS]
```

- `[START_FLASHCARDS]` — begins the flashcard block
- `F::` — the front of a flashcard (the question, term, or prompt)
- `B::` — the back of a flashcard (the answer, definition, or explanation)
- `[END_FLASHCARDS]` — ends the block
- F and B lines are paired: each F is immediately followed by its corresponding B

The AI only includes this block when its system prompt instructs it to. Nothing happens automatically — you have to design the Gem to produce this output.

---

### Adding Flashcard Instructions to a System Prompt

To make a Gem generate flashcards, include instructions in the system prompt. Here's an example section to add:

```
When you explain a concept that the user should memorize — a mechanism, a protocol, a clinical threshold, a terminology definition — include a flashcard block at the end of your response using this exact format:

[START_FLASHCARDS]
F::Question or term (front of card)
B::Answer or explanation (back of card)
[END_FLASHCARDS]

Generate 2–5 flashcards per response when relevant. Not every response needs flashcards — only include them when you've explained something worth memorizing. For conversational questions or planning discussions, omit the flashcard block.
```

Add this section to any Gem where knowledge retention matters: a nutrition coach explaining longevity mechanisms, a recovery coach explaining HRV physiology, a language learning coach, or a study assistant.

---

### What Makes Good Flashcard Content

The AI determines what to create flashcards for based on your system prompt instructions. The best flashcards come from:

**Mechanisms and processes** — How does HRV work? What is the relationship between cortisol and muscle protein synthesis? These are high-value memorization targets.

**Thresholds and reference values** — SpO2 below what percentage is clinically significant? What's a normal resting HRV range? Specific numbers are worth memorizing.

**Protocol steps** — The phases of a deload week, the structure of a periodized training block, the components of an evidence-based sleep protocol.

**Terminology** — Medical, nutrition, or performance science terms you want to retain.

---

### Viewing Generated Flashcards

Flashcards generated by Gems are saved as sets in your Academy Flashcard library. Each set is titled with the date and the Gem that created it. Navigate to the Academy section to find and review them.

Flashcards created this way go into the spaced repetition queue like any other flashcard — you'll be prompted to review them at scheduled intervals.

---

### Controlling Flashcard Frequency

If a Gem is generating too many flashcards (one per response becomes overwhelming), update the system prompt to be more selective: "Only generate flashcards for genuinely high-value memorization targets, not for every concept mentioned. Maximum 3 cards per response."

If it's generating too few or none, check that:
1. The flashcard syntax instructions are in the system prompt
2. The AI is responding to questions where flashcard content would make sense
3. The syntax in your prompt exactly matches the required format

## Key Takeaways

- Flashcards are generated automatically when AI responses include the `[START_FLASHCARDS]...[END_FLASHCARDS]` syntax block
- The system scans responses in the background — no manual action required, no delay to the chat response
- To activate: add flashcard format instructions to the Gem's system prompt
- Format: `F::front` paired with `B::back` inside the delimiters
- Best content: mechanisms, thresholds, protocols, terminology — things worth memorizing
- Generated flashcard sets appear in Academy → Flashcards and enter the spaced repetition queue
- Control frequency via system prompt: instruct the AI on how selective to be with card generation

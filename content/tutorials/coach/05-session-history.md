## Narrator Script

Every coaching session you have is saved. The History panel in the Coach sidebar lets you return to any past conversation and pick up exactly where you left off. This lesson covers how sessions are stored, how to resume them, and how to manage your session list.

---

### The History Panel

In the Coach sidebar, below the Gem selector and New Chat button, there's a **History** section. It lists your past sessions in reverse chronological order (most recent at the top).

Each session entry shows:
- The first 50 characters of your opening message — a preview that helps you identify what that session was about
- The date and time the session was created

Sessions are listed across all your Gems — it's a unified history, not filtered by Gem.

---

### Resuming a Session

Click any session in the History panel. The main chat area loads that session's full conversation history. The AI is ready to continue from where you left off.

When you load a session:
- The Gem selector in the sidebar switches to the Gem associated with that session
- The Gem selector is locked (as it always is for an active session)
- All previous messages in the session are displayed
- Your next message continues the conversation with full context of everything said before

You can resume a session from days or weeks ago and the AI will have the full context of the prior conversation. This is particularly useful for multi-session engagements — a training programming conversation that evolves over several weeks, or a problem-solving session you want to revisit.

---

### Starting a New Chat

Click **New Chat** in the sidebar. This:
- Clears the current chat display
- Resets the Gem selector to its default (first Gem in your list)
- Allows you to select a different Gem for the new session
- Does not delete the session you were just in — it remains in History

Use New Chat whenever you're starting a genuinely different conversation. Keeping unrelated topics in the same session degrades context quality — the AI tries to maintain coherence across the full message history, so a session about nutrition that suddenly shifts to training programming will produce less focused responses than two separate sessions.

---

### Session Naming

Sessions are not manually named. The title shown in History is automatically derived from the first message you send. For this reason, your first message doubles as the session title — a specific, descriptive opening message is easier to find later than a vague one.

Compare:
- "Tell me about nutrition" → hard to identify later
- "Evaluate my current nutrition protocol for longevity: high protein (220g), low saturated fat, intermittent fasting 16:8" → immediately identifiable

You don't need to change how you message to accommodate this — just write specific first messages and your History will be easy to navigate.

---

### No Deletion in the Coach

Sessions cannot be deleted from the Coach interface. The History is a permanent record of your AI coaching conversations. Over time, this history becomes a useful reference: you can see how your coaching conversations have evolved, what questions you were asking six months ago, and what advice you received at different stages of your journey.

## Key Takeaways

- History panel in sidebar lists all past sessions by first message preview + date
- Click any session to resume it — full context loads, AI continues from where you left off
- Gem selector switches to the session's Gem and locks when a session is loaded
- New Chat clears the display and unlocks the Gem selector — prior session is preserved in History
- Sessions are named by your first message — specific opening messages = navigable history
- Sessions cannot be deleted — History is a permanent record of your coaching conversations

## Narrator Script

CentenarianOS is a personal operating system designed to help you track, plan, and optimize the things that matter — your time, money, health, travel, nutrition, and learning. Everything lives in one platform, and everything connects.

This lesson gives you a high-level overview of what the platform does and how it's organized.

---

### The Philosophy

Most productivity tools do one thing well but force you to jump between apps. CentenarianOS brings everything under one roof. Your financial data connects to your travel logs. Your health metrics connect to your focus sessions. Your meal tracking connects to your recipes. The idea is that cross-referencing your own data reveals patterns you'd never see in isolated apps.

The name comes from the goal of living well past 100 — and the operating system metaphor reflects how every module in the platform works together, like subsystems in an OS.

---

### The Five Navigation Groups

Your dashboard sidebar organizes every module into five groups:

**Operate** — the productivity core.
- Daily Tasks — your daily planner with week/3-day/daily views
- Engine — focus timer, daily debrief, pain tracking
- Weekly Review — AI-generated summaries of your week
- Roadmap — multi-level goal hierarchy (Roadmap → Goal → Milestone → Task)

**Health** — your body and performance data.
- Fuel — ingredient library, meal logging, NCV (Nutrient Cost Value) framework
- Metrics — resting heart rate, steps, sleep, activity minutes, body composition
- Wearables — Oura, WHOOP, Garmin OAuth sync + CSV imports (Apple Health, Google Health, InBody, Hume)
- Workouts — exercise tracking
- Correlations — cross-metric analysis
- Analytics — trends and insights

**Life** — money, movement, and gear.
- Finance — accounts, transactions, budgets, brands, CSV import/export
- Travel — vehicles, fuel logs, trips, maintenance, Garmin imports, bike savings
- Equipment — catalog your gear, track value, link purchases, cross-module connections

**Learn** — knowledge and community.
- Blog — write and publish articles
- Recipes — create, import, and share recipes
- Academy — take courses, track progress, CYOA navigation
- Live — streaming sessions

**AI** — intelligent assistants (admin-only for now).
- Coach — AI-powered coaching
- Gems — AI data consultant

---

### Free vs. Paid

Some parts of CentenarianOS are free for everyone:
- Blog and recipes (reading and publishing)
- Academy courses (browsing and taking free courses)
- Live sessions

Everything else requires a paid plan — Monthly ($10/mo) or Lifetime ($100 one-time). The next lesson covers pricing in detail.

### Open Source

CentenarianOS is open source under the MIT License. The code is on GitHub, and contributions are welcome. The `/tech-roadmap` page shows the full product roadmap with completed, in-progress, and planned phases.

## Key Takeaways

- CentenarianOS is a personal operating system — productivity, health, finance, travel, learning, and AI in one platform
- Five nav groups: Operate, Health, Life, Learn, AI
- Cross-module connections let you link data between modules for deeper insights
- Blog, recipes, academy, and live sessions are free; everything else requires a paid plan
- Open source on GitHub with a public tech roadmap at /tech-roadmap

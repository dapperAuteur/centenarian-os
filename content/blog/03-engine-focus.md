# Engine: Focus Better, Work Smarter, and Reflect Every Day

Have you ever worked for hours but felt like you got nothing done? That happens when you do not track your focus. The Engine helps you work in short, powerful bursts and look back at your day with clear eyes.

## What Is It?

The Engine is your daily work tracker. It has three parts that work together:

- **Focus Timer** — A timer that helps you work in focused blocks of time. Like a sprint for your brain.
- **Daily Debrief** — A short check-in at the end of the day. You rate how your day went and write down what you learned.
- **Pain Tracking** — A simple way to check in on how your body feels. You log where you hurt and how bad it is.

Together, these three tools show you the full picture of your day — how much you worked, how well it went, and how your body is holding up.

## Why Does It Help You?

- Short focus sessions help your brain work better than long, scattered ones.
- You can track how much money your work is worth per hour.
- The daily debrief helps you learn from each day instead of just forgetting it.
- Tracking body pain helps you spot patterns — like "my back always hurts on heavy work days."
- Weekly summaries show you how many hours you focused over the whole week.

## How To Use It — Step by Step

1. Go to your dashboard and click **Engine**.
2. To start working, click **Focus Timer**. Set your session topic and start the timer.
3. Work without stopping until the timer ends.
4. Log how much the session was worth if you are tracking revenue.
5. At the end of the day, click **Daily Debrief**. Rate your day and write a few notes.
6. Click **Pain Tracking** to check in on your body. Mark any spots that hurt and how much.

## A Quick Example

You sit down to write a report. You open Engine and start a 25-minute focus session called "Write report intro." You work without checking your phone. When the timer goes off, you take a 5-minute break. You do this four times. At the end of the day, you go to Daily Debrief and give your day a 4 out of 5. You note that your neck felt tight. Over the next few weeks, you notice your neck always hurts when you sit too long. Now you know to take stretch breaks.

## Ready to Try It?

Try one focus session today. Set the timer for 25 minutes. Pick one thing to work on. Do nothing else until it goes off. You might surprise yourself.

# Recipes: Save, Share, and Discover Meals That Fuel Your Best Life

A good recipe is a treasure. The Recipes module helps you save all your favorite meals, share them with others, and discover new ones from people who care about eating well.

## What Is It?

The Recipes module is your personal cookbook — but it lives online and connects to a whole community. You save recipes, add the ingredients, and share them with other members. Other members can save and like your recipes too.

Here is what you get:

- **Your Recipes** — Every recipe you have saved or created, all in one place.
- **Recipe Builder** — A tool to build a recipe step by step, with ingredients and instructions.
- **Your Cook Page** — A public page where others can see all your recipes.
- **Discovery** — Browse recipes from other members of the community.
- **Liked and Saved** — Quick access to recipes from other people that you want to make again.
- **Analytics** — See which of your recipes people love most.

Recipes also connect to the Fuel module. When you build a recipe in Recipes, it links to your ingredient library and shows you the full nutrition info automatically.

## Why Does It Help You?

- You never lose a recipe again. It is saved and safe.
- You can share recipes with family and friends using just a link.
- You learn from other members what meals work for long, healthy living.
- Nutrition info is built in — you know what you are eating before you cook it.
- You can import a recipe from any website with just a link — no typing needed.

## How To Use It — Step by Step

1. Go to your dashboard and click **Recipes**.
2. To add a recipe from a website, click **Import**. Paste the URL. The recipe fills in automatically.
3. To build your own, click **New Recipe**. Add a name, add your ingredients, and write the steps.
4. Click **Publish** to share it on your public cook page.
5. To discover new recipes, browse the community section.
6. Like or save any recipe you want to try or keep.

## A Quick Example

You make an amazing lemon garlic chicken every Sunday. You decide to save it in Recipes. You add each ingredient, the amounts, and the cooking steps. The app shows you it has 42 grams of protein per serving. You publish it. Three other members save it. One of them tries it and leaves a comment saying it was their best meal of the month. Your one recipe helped three other people eat well.

## Ready to Try It?

Start with one recipe you already know by heart. Build it in the Recipe Builder. You will have your first recipe saved in under five minutes.

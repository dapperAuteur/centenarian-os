# Health Metrics: See What Your Body Is Really Telling You

Your body sends you signals every single day. Your heart rate, your sleep, your steps. But most people never look at these numbers together. The Health Metrics module shows you the full picture.

## What Is It?

Health Metrics is like a report card for your body. You plug in numbers from your smartwatch, fitness tracker, or scale. The app keeps track of them over time. Then it shows you trends — which means it shows you if things are getting better or worse.

There are three types of data you can track:

- **Core Metrics** — Resting heart rate, steps per day, sleep hours, and active minutes.
- **Enrichment Metrics** — Deeper data like sleep quality score, HRV (heart rate variability), blood oxygen, stress levels, and recovery score.
- **Body Composition** — Weight, body fat percentage, muscle mass, and BMI.

HRV stands for heart rate variability. It tells you how well your heart bounces back between beats. A high HRV usually means your body is recovered and ready to go. A low HRV means you might need more rest.

## Why Does It Help You?

- You stop guessing and start knowing how your body is doing.
- You can spot patterns — like "I sleep better when I walk 8,000 steps."
- The 7-day average smooths out random bad days so you see the real trend.
- You can import data from your device instead of typing it all by hand.
- Some metrics unlock as you learn more, so you are never overwhelmed at the start.

## How To Use It — Step by Step

1. Go to your dashboard and click **Metrics**.
2. Start with the core metrics — heart rate, steps, sleep, and active minutes.
3. Each day, enter your numbers. You can type them in or import from your device.
4. Check the 7-day averages to see your trend.
5. As you get comfortable, unlock more metrics like sleep score and HRV.
6. Look for patterns. Does your sleep affect your next day's steps? Find out.

## A Quick Example

You start logging your sleep hours every night. After two weeks, you notice you average only 6 hours. The healthy goal is 7 to 9 hours. You also notice your resting heart rate is higher on days after poor sleep. You decide to go to bed 30 minutes earlier. After one month, your sleep average goes up to 7.2 hours and your heart rate drops. Your body is telling you it feels better. You can see the proof.

## Ready to Try It?

Start with just one number today. Log your steps or your sleep hours. That one number, tracked every day, will teach you more about your health than you expect.

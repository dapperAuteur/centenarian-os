# Fuel: Know Exactly What You Are Eating and Why It Matters

Food is the fuel your body runs on. Good fuel means more energy. Bad fuel means feeling tired and sick. The Fuel module helps you know exactly what goes into your body every day.

## What Is It?

Fuel is your personal food tracker. It is like having a nutrition label for your whole life. You can save recipes, plan your meals, track what you cook in bulk, and keep an eye on what food you have at home. It all works together so you always know what you are eating and how much it costs.

Fuel has five sections:

- **Ingredients** — A list of every food item you use, with nutrition facts and cost.
- **Recipes** — Meals you have saved, with all the ingredients inside.
- **Meal Prep** — When you cook a big batch, you track how many servings it makes.
- **Meals** — What you actually ate today.
- **Inventory** — What food you have at home right now.

## Why Does It Help You?

- You stop guessing what is in your food. You know.
- You find out if you are getting enough protein, vitamins, or calories.
- You save money because you track food costs.
- You never forget what you have in your fridge.
- You get a warning when you are running low on something important.

## How To Use It — Step by Step

1. Go to your dashboard and click **Fuel**.
2. Click **Ingredients** and add foods you use often. Put in the nutrition info from the package.
3. Click **Recipes** and build your favorite meals using those ingredients.
4. When you cook a big batch, go to **Meal Prep** and log how many servings you made.
5. Each day, go to **Meals** and log what you ate. Pick from your saved recipes or add a quick entry.
6. Check **Inventory** to see what you are running low on.

## A Quick Example

You make a big pot of chicken and rice on Sunday. You go to Meal Prep and log it as 8 servings. Each day that week, you log one serving under Meals. By Friday, Fuel tells you that you ate well and spent only $1.50 per meal. It also warns you that you are almost out of olive oil. You add it to your shopping list before you run out.

## Ready to Try It?

Start by adding three foods you eat every week. Then build one recipe. You will be amazed at what you learn about what you eat.

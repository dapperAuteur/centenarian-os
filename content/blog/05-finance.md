# Finance: Know Where Your Money Goes Every Month

Most people have no idea where their money goes. It just disappears. The Finance module helps you see every dollar — what comes in and what goes out — so you stay in control.

## What Is It?

The Finance module is like a piggy bank you can read. It shows you all your spending and income in one place. You can sort it by category — like food, rent, or fun money. You can set a budget for each category. And you can see charts that make your money easy to understand.

Here is what you get:

- **Summary Cards** — A quick view of your monthly expenses, income, and what is left over.
- **Spending Chart** — A bar chart showing how much you spent each month.
- **Category Chart** — A pie chart showing where your money goes (food, bills, fun, etc.).
- **Budget Bars** — For each category, a bar that fills up as you spend toward your limit.
- **Transaction History** — Every purchase and payment you have logged.

## Why Does It Help You?

- You stop being surprised when money runs out.
- You can set a budget and see when you are getting close to the limit.
- Charts make money easy to understand at a glance.
- You can see if your income is growing or shrinking over months.
- You can export your data to share with an accountant or to save for your records.

## How To Use It — Step by Step

1. Go to your dashboard and click **Finance**.
2. Set up your categories. Examples: Food, Rent, Transportation, Fun.
3. Set a monthly budget for each category. Example: $500 for food.
4. Each time you spend money, click **Add Transaction** and log it.
5. Pick the category it belongs to. Add the amount and a note.
6. Check your budget bars to see how close you are to your limits.
7. At the end of the month, look at the charts to see the full picture.

## A Quick Example

You set a food budget of $400 per month. You log every grocery trip and restaurant meal. By the 20th of the month, your food bar is already at 90%. You realize you have been eating out too much. For the last ten days, you cook at home. You end the month at $395 — just under budget. Over six months, you save $300 just by watching that one category.

## Ready to Try It?

Add one transaction right now. It does not have to be big. Just log one purchase from today. That first log is the start of knowing your money.

# Travel Log: See How Every Mile Shapes Your Health and Wallet

Every trip you take tells a story. A bike ride to work is exercise you did not have to schedule. A fill-up at the pump is money leaving your wallet. A train ticket is a chance to read instead of drive. The Travel module connects all of it.

## What Is It?

The Travel module tracks every way you get around — bike, car, bus, train, plane, walking, and more. It connects to your Finance module for costs and your Health Metrics for activity. It gives you a clear picture of how your movement habits affect your body, your budget, and the planet.

It has four main sections:

- **Trip Log** — Record any trip by mode, distance, duration, and purpose
- **Fuel Log** — Track every car fill-up with odometer, MPG, gallons, and cost
- **Maintenance Log** — Log service records for your car and bike
- **Import** — Bring in years of Garmin activity data or scan old dashboard photos

## Why Does It Help You?

- Bike commuting is free exercise. The Travel module shows you exactly how many calories you burned getting to work.
- Every bike trip has a dollar value. The module calculates what that trip would have cost by car — and shows you the savings.
- Fuel costs add up fast. Tracking fill-ups reveals your true cost per mile and whether your MPG is trending up or down.
- Your Weekly Review includes a Travel summary so your coach can connect commute habits to energy and recovery trends.

## How To Use It — Step by Step

**Step 1: Add your vehicles**
Start by registering your car and bike on the Travel dashboard. Give each a nickname. Your car needs a year, make, and model. Your bike just needs a name.

**Step 2: Set your commute defaults**
Go to Import > Commute Settings and enter your one-way commute distance and typical duration. Now the "Log Commute" button on the dashboard fills everything in automatically. One tap, done.

**Step 3: Log your first trip**
Hit "Add Trip," pick a mode, enter the distance and duration, and choose a purpose. For bike commutes, just tap "Log Commute" — it uses your saved defaults.

**Step 4: Log a fuel fill-up**
Go to the Fuel Log page. Tap "Scan Photos" and select the dashboard photos you took at the pump. The AI reads Trip A (miles since last fill), Trip B (miles this month), the odometer, MPG, gallons, total cost, and price per gallon — then fills in the form for you. Review, adjust if needed, and save.

**Step 5: Import your Garmin history**
Go to Import > Garmin Activities CSV. Export your activity history from Garmin Connect and upload the file. The module imports cycling, walking, running, and hiking trips automatically — skipping pure gym activities. Duplicates are detected so you can re-import safely.

## A Quick Example

Suppose you bike to work 5 days a week, 3.5 miles each way. Your car costs about $0.18 per mile in gas. In one month of bike commuting (22 days, 154 miles round trip), you burned roughly 1,230 calories just commuting — and saved about $28 in gas. Over a year that is $336 in your pocket and the equivalent of 24 extra workouts you did not have to plan.

The Travel module makes that visible so you can see the compound effect of small daily choices.

## Ready to Try It?

Open the Travel dashboard and start with two things: add your vehicles and log your next commute. If you have been photographing your dashboard at fill-ups, head to Fuel Log and scan a few photos to see how quickly the AI extracts the data. Your mileage history has been waiting to tell you something.
